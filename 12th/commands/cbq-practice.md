# /cbq-practice Command — The Competency Engine

Competency-Based Questions (CBQs) now account for **50% of the CBSE 12th Grade paper**. These questions don't test "what you know" but "how you apply what you know" to new situations.

## Command Syntax
```
/cbq-practice [subject] [chapter] [count]
```

## What is a CBQ?

In the CBSE 2026-27 pattern, a CBQ consists of:
1. **A Stimulus:** A paragraph of text, a data table, a complex circuit diagram, or a case study about a real-world discovery.
2. **Sub-Questions:** 3-4 questions based on the stimulus.
   - 1-2 MCQs for quick check.
   - 1 SA (2M) for conceptual application.
   - 1 Case-based numerical (if science/math).

---

## High-Yield CBQ Topics

### 🔭 Physics
- **Electrostatics:** Van de Graaff generator or Lightning conductors.
- **Current Electricity:** Electricity billing or Household circuit safety.
- **Optics:** Human eye defects or Telescope setups in observatories.
- **Semiconductors:** Solar cells or LED efficiency.

### 🧪 Chemistry
- **Solutions:** Scuba diving (Henry's law) or Anti-freeze in cars.
- **Electrochemistry:** Lead-acid batteries or Hydrogen fuel cells.
- **Biomolecules:** DNA structure discovery or Enzyme kinetics.

### 📐 Mathematics
- **Calculus:** Maxima/Minima in manufacturing (box dimensions).
- **Probability:** Quality control in factories or Medical test accuracy (Bayes').
- **LPP:** Optimization of profit in a transport business.

### 🧬 Biology
- **Genetics:** Pedigree charts of a family or DNA fingerprinting case.
- **Biotechnology:** Insulin production flow or Bt Cotton environmental impact.
- **Ecology:** Biodiversity loss data or Food web perturbations.

---

## Command Output Format

1. **The Case Study:** A 150-200 word passage or a complex ASCII/Mermaid diagram.
2. **Sub-questions:** 4 targeted questions.
3. **The "Examiner's View":** Why this specific case is high-yield for boards.
4. **Marking Scheme:** Exact value points for each sub-question.

---
