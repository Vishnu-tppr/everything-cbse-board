# Word Budget Rules — The Detail Protocol

These rules ensure that the AI provides the "Senior Sibling" level of detail (200-400 words typical for complex topics) without becoming repetitive or vague.

## 1. Detail over Brevity

- **Rule:** For Grade 12, "Simple" is often "Incomplete." 
- ✅ **DO:** Provide the intuition AND the math AND the exam context.
- ❌ **DON'T:** Give a one-sentence definition for a 3-mark concept.

---

## 2. The 300-Word Benchmark

For a standard conceptual explanation (e.g., "Nernst Equation"):
- **50 words:** Intuition and real-world example.
- **100 words:** The Derivation and Variable definition.
- **100 words:** Example calculation or application.
- **50 words:** Board exam "Value Points" and traps.

---

## 3. Formatting for Scannability

Even with high detail, the output must be readable:
- Use **Bold** for keywords.
- Use **Lists** for steps/properties.
- Use **Tables** for comparisons (e.g., SN1 vs SN2).
- Use **Blockquotes** for "Topper Tips".

---

## 4. Constraint-Based Conciseness

- For 1-mark MCQs: Keep it under 50 words.
- For 5-mark LA: Provide a detailed breakdown of at least 400-500 words (including steps).

---

## 5. Avoiding "Fluff"

- Detailed does NOT mean wordy. 
- Every sentence must add either **Conceptual Value**, **Mathematical Logic**, or **Exam Strategy**.
- If a sentence doesn't help the student score a mark or understand the "Why", delete it.

---

## 6. The "Example Mandate"

- No abstract explanation is complete without at least ONE concrete numerical or case-based example.
- This example adds "High-Quality Detail" that students find most useful.
