# 🧬 Ch05 - Principles of Inheritance and Variation

> **Biology** | **Marks**: ~10-12 | [[🧬 Biology]]
> This chapter covers Mendelian genetics, chromosomal theory of inheritance, linkage, and genetic disorders.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Mendel’s Laws
- **Dominance**: In a heterozygous pair, one allele expresses itself.
- **Segregation**: Alleles separate during gamete formation ($3:1$ phenotype in $F_2$).
- **Independent Assortment**: Traits inherit independently ($9:3:3:1$ in dihybrid).

### 2. Post-Mendelian Genetics
- **Incomplete Dominance**: intermediate phenotype (e.g., Pink snapdragon). Ratio: $1:2:1$ for both geno and phenotype.
- **Co-dominance**: Both alleles express equally (e.g., ABO Blood Groups).
- **Multiple Allelism**: More than two alleles for a trait (e.g., $I^A, I^B, i$ for blood groups).

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Linkage & Recombination (T.H. Morgan)
- **Linkage**: Physical association of genes on a chromosome.
- **Recombination**: Crossing over during meiosis.
- **Distance**: Recombination frequency is proportional to the distance between genes (used for gene mapping).

### ⚡ Sex Determination & Mutation
- **Humans**: $XY$ mechanism. Male is heterogametic.
- **Birds**: $ZW$ mechanism. Female is heterogametic.
- **Mutations**: Point mutation (e.g., Sickle cell anemia) and Frame-shift mutations.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Test Cross**: Crossing $F_1$ with the **homozygous recessive** parent to find the genotype.
- **Pedigree Analysis**: Squares are males, circles are females. Shaded = affected.
- **Genetic Disorders**:
  - **Mendelian**: Hemophilia (X-linked), Sickle cell (Autosomal), Phenylketonuria.
  - **Chromosomal**: Down’s ($21^{st}$ trisomy), Klinefelter’s ($XXY$), Turner’s ($XO$).
