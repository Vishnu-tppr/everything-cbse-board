# 🌸 Ch02 - Sexual Reproduction in Flowering Plants

> **Biology** | **Marks**: ~6 | [[🧬 Biology]]
> This chapter covers the structures and processes of sexual reproduction in Angiosperms, from gametogenesis to seed formation.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Pre-fertilization: Structures & Events
- **Stamen (Androecium)**: 
  - Microsporogenesis: Formation of microspores (pollen grains) from PMC.
  - Pollen grain: Exine (Sporopollenin - most resistant organic material) and Intine.
- **Pistil (Gynoecium)**:
  - Megasporogenesis: Formation of megaspores from MMC.
  - Embryo sac (Female Gametophyte): **7-celled, 8-nucleate** structure.

### 2. Pollination
- **Autogamy**: Self-pollination in same flower.
- **Geitonogamy**: Different flower, same plant (Genetically autogamy, functionally cross).
- **Xenogamy**: Different plant (Brings genetic variation).
- **Agents**: Biotic (Insects, birds) and Abiotic (Wind, Water).

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Double Fertilization (Unique to Angiosperms)
1. **Syngamy**: One male gamete + Egg $\to$ Zygote ($2n$).
2. **Triple Fusion**: Second male gamete + 2 Polar nuclei $\to$ PEN ($3n$).
- **Result**: Zygote becomes Embryo; PEN becomes Endosperm (nourishes embryo).

### ⚡ Post-fertilization Events
- **Endosperm development** precedes embryo development.
- **Seed**: Mature ovule. Can be Albuminous (with endosperm, e.g., wheat, maize) or Non-albuminous (without endosperm, e.g., pea, bean).
- **Fruit**: Mature ovary. Pericarp is the fruit wall.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Sporopollenin**: No enzyme can degrade it. This is why pollen are well-preserved as fossils.
- **Egg Apparatus**: Consists of 2 Synergids and 1 Egg cell.
- **Apomixis**: Production of seeds without fertilization (Mimics sexual reproduction).
- **Polyembryony**: Presence of more than one embryo in a seed (Common in Citrus).
