# 🌍 Ch13 - Organisms and Populations

> **Biology** | **Marks**: ~4 | [[🧬 Biology]]
> This chapter examines how organisms adapt to their environment and the dynamics of population growth and interactions.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Adaptation
- **Kangaroo Rat**: Internal fat oxidation to produce water; concentrated urine.
- **Desert Plants (Opuntia)**: Thick cuticle, sunken stomata, leaf reduced to spines.
- **Altitude Sickness**: Compensated by increasing RBC production, increasing breathing rate, and decreasing binding affinity of Hemoglobin.
- **Ectotherms (Lizards)**: Behavioral adaptations (basking in sun).

### 2. Population Attributes
- **Birth/Death rate**: Expressed as per capita.
- **Sex Ratio**: Percentage of males and females.
- **Age Pyramids**: Expanding (triangular), Stable (bell-shaped), Declining (urn-shaped).

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Population Growth Models
1. **Exponential Growth**: $dN/dt = rN$. Occurs when resources are unlimited. J-shaped curve.
2. **Logistic Growth**: $dN/dt = rN [(K-N)/K]$. Occurs when resources are limited ($K = \text{Carrying Capacity}$). S-shaped (Sigmoid) curve. More realistic.

### ⚡ Population Interactions
- **Mutualism ($+,+$)**: Lichens, Mycorrhiza, Fig-Wasp.
- **Competition ($-,-$)**: Gause's Principle (no two species can occupy same niche indefinitely).
- **Predation ($+,-$)**: Maintains species diversity.
- **Parasitism ($+,-$)**: Ectoparasites (Lice) and Endoparasites (Tapeworm).
- **Commensalism ($+,0$)**: Orchid on mango tree, Barnacles on whale.
- **Amensalism ($-,0$)**: Penicillium inhibiting bacteria.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Brood Parasitism**: Cuckoo laying eggs in Crow’s nest. The crow hatches them.
- **Verhulst-Pearl Logistic Growth**: The official name for the Sigmoid growth model.
- **Eurythermal vs Stenothermal**: Eury (wide range of temp), Steno (narrow range).
- **Allen’s Rule**: Mammals from colder climates have shorter ears and limbs to minimize heat loss.
