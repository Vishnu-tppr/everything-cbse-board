# 🧬 Ch12 - Biotechnology and its Applications

> **Biology** | **Marks**: ~6 | [[🧬 Biology]]
> This chapter covers how biotechnology is applied in medicine, agriculture, and industry, along with ethical and safety considerations.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Applications in Agriculture
- **GM Crops**: Increased tolerance to abiotic stress, reduced reliance on chemical pesticides.
- **Bt Cotton**: *Bacillus thuringiensis* produces toxic proteins (*Cry* proteins).
  - *CryIAc* and *CryIIAb* control cotton bollworms.
  - *CryIAb* controls corn borer.
- **RNA Interference (RNAi)**: Used to prevent *Meloidegyne incognita* infection in tobacco roots.

### 2. Applications in Medicine
- **Genetically Engineered Insulin**: Produced by Eli Lilly (1983). Pro-insulin contains C-peptide (removed in mature insulin). $A$ and $B$ chains are linked by Disulfide bridges.
- **Gene Therapy**: First clinical use in 1990 for a 4-year-old girl with **ADA deficiency**.
- **Molecular Diagnosis**: Using PCR (detecting low-level pathogens) and ELISA (detecting antigens/antibodies).

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Transgenic Animals
- Animals with manipulated DNA. **Mice** (95% of all transgenic animals).
- **Rosie (1997)**: First transgenic cow. Produced milk rich in human protein ($\alpha$-lactalbumin).
- **Uses**: Testing vaccine safety, chemical toxicity, and studying normal physiology/diseases.

### ⚡ Ethical Issues
- **GEAC**: Genetic Engineering Approval Committee (Indian body to monitor GM research safety).
- **Biopiracy**: Use of bio-resources by MNCs without authorization (e.g., Basmati rice patents).

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **C-peptide**: Absent in mature human insulin.
- **Bt Toxin Activation**: Occurs in the **alkaline pH** of the insect gut. It is inactive in the bacteria.
- **RNAi Method**: Uses double-stranded RNA (dsRNA) to silence specific mRNA.
- **ADA Deficiency**: Caused by the deletion of the gene for Adenosine Deaminase. Permanent cure involves gene therapy in the early embryonic stage.
