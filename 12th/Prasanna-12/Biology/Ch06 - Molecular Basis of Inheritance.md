# 🧬 Ch06 - Molecular Basis of Inheritance

> **Biology** | **Marks**: ~10-12 | [[🧬 Biology]]
> This chapter covers the molecular nature of genetic material, DNA replication, gene expression (transcription/translation), and the Human Genome Project.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. DNA Structure & Packaging
- **DNA**: Polymer of nucleotides (Sugar + Phosphate + Base). Double helix (Watson & Crick).
- **Packaging**: DNA is negatively charged; wrapped around positively charged **Histone octamers** to form **Nucleosomes**.
- **Chromatin**: Euchromatin (lightly packed, active) vs Heterochromatin (densely packed, inactive).

### 2. Search for Genetic Material
- **Griffith (1928)**: Transforming Principle (S-strain vs R-strain).
- **Avery, MacLeod, McCarty**: Proved DNA is the transforming principle.
- **Hershey & Chase (1952)**: Unequivocal proof using bacteriophages and radioactive isotopes ($^{32}P, ^{35}S$).

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 The Central Dogma
1. **Replication**: Semiconservative (Meselson & Stahl experiment). Enzymes: DNA Polymerase, Helicase, Ligase.
2. **Transcription**: DNA $\to$ RNA. Only one strand is copied. Enzymes: RNA Polymerase. 
   - Processing (Eukaryotes): Splicing (remove introns), Capping, Tailing.
3. **Translation**: RNA $\to$ Protein. Ribosomes and tRNA.

### ⚡ Gene Regulation & HGP
- **Lac Operon**: Jacob and Monod. Regulated by a repressor. In presence of Inducer (Lactose), repressor is inactivated.
- **Human Genome Project (HGP)**: "Mega project". 3 billion base pairs. Chromosome 1 has most genes; Y has fewest.
- **DNA Fingerprinting**: Uses **VNTRs** (Variable Number Tandem Repeats).

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Semi-conservative Proof**: Meselson and Stahl used $^{15}N$ (Heavy Nitrogen), NOT radioactive nitrogen.
- **Genetic Code**: Universal, degenerate, non-overlapping, and unambiguous. Start codon is **AUG** (codes for Methionine).
- **DNA Polymerase Direction**: Synthesizes only in $5' \to 3'$ direction. Leads to leading and lagging (Okazaki fragments) strands.
