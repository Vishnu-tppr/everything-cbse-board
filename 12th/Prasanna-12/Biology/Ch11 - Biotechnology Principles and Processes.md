# 🧬 Ch11 - Biotechnology Principles and Processes

> **Biology** | **Marks**: ~6 | [[🧬 Biology]]
> This chapter introduces the tools and techniques of genetic engineering, from restriction enzymes to large-scale production in bioreactors.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Tools of r-DNA Technology
- **Restriction Enzymes**: "Molecular scissors". Cut at specific **Palindromic sequences**.
  - **EcoRI**: First letter (Genus), next two (Species), strain, and order of discovery.
- **DNA Ligase**: "Molecular glue".
- **Cloning Vectors**: DNA molecule used to carry foreign DNA. 
  - Features: Origin of replication (**ori**), Selectable marker ($amp^R, tet^R$), Cloning sites.
  - **pBR322**: Most common vector.

### 2. Processes of r-DNA Technology
- **Isolation of DNA**: Using Chilled Ethanol.
- **Amplification (PCR)**: Denaturation ($94^\circ C$), Annealing, Extension ($72^\circ C$). Uses **Taq Polymerase**.
- **Insertion**: Transforming into host cells (using heat shock, micro-injection, or biolistics/gene gun).

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Selectable Markers
- Used to distinguish **Transformants** from non-transformants.
- **Inactivation mechanism**: If foreign DNA is inserted into the $tet^R$ gene, the plasmid loses tetracycline resistance.
- **Blue-White Screening**: Insertional inactivation of $\beta$-galactosidase gene. Non-recombinants turn blue in X-gal.

### ⚡ Bioreactors
- Large vessels ($100-1000$ liters) for large-scale production.
- **Stirred-tank bioreactor**: Most common; provides better aeration and mixing.
- **Downstream Processing**: Separation and purification of the product before marketing.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **PCR Enzyme**: Taq Polymerase is used because it is **thermostable** (isolated from *Thermus aquaticus*).
- **Sticky vs Blunt ends**: Sticky ends are preferred for ligation as they facilitate base pairing.
- **Agarose Gel Electrophoresis**: DNA is negatively charged and moves towards the **Anode**. Smaller fragments move faster/further.
- **Ethidium Bromide**: Used to visualize DNA under UV light (Orange bands).
