# 🌿 Ch15 - Biodiversity and Conservation

> **Biology** | **Marks**: ~3 | [[🧬 Biology]]
> This chapter emphasizes the importance of biodiversity, reasons for its loss, and the strategies for its conservation.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Levels of Biodiversity (Edward Wilson)
- **Genetic Diversity**: Variation within a species (e.g., *Rauwolfia vomitoria* potency).
- **Species Diversity**: Variation at species level (e.g., Western Ghats have more amphibians than Eastern Ghats).
- **Ecological Diversity**: Variation at ecosystem level (e.g., Deserts, Rain forests).

### 2. Species Patterns
- **Latitudinal Gradients**: Species diversity decreases as we move away from the equator.
- **Species-Area Relationship (Alexander von Humboldt)**: $S = C A^z$. On a log scale, it’s a straight line: $\log S = \log C + Z \log A$. 
  - $Z$ (slope) is usually $0.1$ to $0.2$, but for large continents, it is $0.6$ to $1.2$.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Loss of Biodiversity (The "Evil Quartet")
1. **Habitat Loss & Fragmentation**: Most important cause (e.g., Amazon rain forest - lungs of the planet).
2. **Over-exploitation**: Humans harvesting more than can be replaced (e.g., Steller’s sea cow).
3. **Alien Species Invasion**: E.g., Nile perch in Lake Victoria, Parthenium (carrot grass), Lantana.
4. **Co-extinctions**: When a species becomes extinct, the species associated with it also become extinct.

### ⚡ Conservation Strategies
- **In-situ (On-site)**: Protecting the entire ecosystem. 
  - Biosphere Reserves, National Parks, Sanctuaries, Sacred Groves.
  - **Hotspots**: High species richness and high degree of endemism (currently 34).
- **Ex-situ (Off-site)**: Protecting threatened species in special settings.
  - Zoological parks, Botanical gardens, Cryopreservation (seed banks, tissue culture).

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Endemism**: Species found in a specific region and nowhere else.
- **Rivet Popper Hypothesis (Paul Ehrlich)**: Explains how loss of a species (rivet) affects the ecosystem (airplane). Loss of key species (wing rivets) is catastrophic.
- **Earth Summit (1992)**: Rio de Janeiro (Biodiversity conservation).
- **World Summit (2002)**: Johannesburg (Sustainable development).
