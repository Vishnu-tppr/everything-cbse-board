# 🌲 Ch14 - Ecosystem

> **Biology** | **Marks**: ~3 | [[🧬 Biology]]
> This chapter focuses on the structure and function of ecosystems, productivity, decomposition, and energy flow across trophic levels.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Ecosystem Structure
- **Components**: Productivity, Decomposition, Energy Flow, Nutrient Cycling.
- **Stratification**: Vertical distribution of different species (e.g., trees at top, shrubs middle, herbs bottom).

### 2. Productivity & Decomposition
- **Productivity**: 
  - **GPP (Gross Primary Productivity)**: Rate of production of organic matter.
  - **NPP (Net Primary Productivity)**: GPP minus Respiration losses ($R$). $NPP = GPP - R$.
- **Decomposition**: Breaking down complex organic matter. 
  - **Steps**: Fragmentation, Leaching, Catabolism, Humification, Mineralization.
  - **Factor**: Lignin and Chitin rich detritus decompose slowly.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Energy Flow
- **10% Law**: Only 10% of energy is transferred to the next trophic level.
- **Food Chain**: GFC (Grazing) vs DFC (Detritus). In aquatic ecosystems, GFC is the major conduit. In terrestrial, DFC is major.

### ⚡ Ecological Pyramids
- **Pyramid of Number**: Usually upright (except in a tree ecosystem).
- **Pyramid of Biomass**: Upright on land; **Inverted in Sea** (phytoplankton biomass is less than fish).
- **Pyramid of Energy**: **ALWAYS UPRIGHT** (energy is lost as heat at each step).

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Net Primary Productivity (NPP)**: This is the actual biomass available for consumption by heterotrophs.
- **Decomposition rate**: Faster in warm, moist environments with Nitrogen-rich detritus.
- **Limitation of Pyramids**: They do not account for the same species belonging to two or more trophic levels and don't include saprophytes.
