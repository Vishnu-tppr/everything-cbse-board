# 🌐 Ch08 - Computer Networks - I

> **Computer Science** | **Marks**: ~5 | [[💻 Computer Science]]
> This chapter covers the basics of networking, types of networks, transmission media, and network topologies.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Types of Networks
- **PAN**: Personal Area (Bluetooth range).
- **LAN**: Local Area (Building/Office).
- **MAN**: Metropolitan Area (City).
- **WAN**: Wide Area (Global - Internet).

### 2. Transmission Media
- **Wired**: 
  - Twisted Pair (cheap, short range).
  - Coaxial (cable TV).
  - Fiber Optic (very fast, uses light, expensive).
- **Wireless**: Infrared, Radio waves, Microwaves, Satellite.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Network Topologies
- **Star**: All nodes connected to a central hub/switch. Easy to install.
- **Bus**: All nodes on a single cable. Collision risk.
- **Tree**: Hierarchical structure.
- **Mesh**: Every node connected to every other node. High reliability.

### ⚡ Network Devices
- **Modem**: Modulator-Demodulator.
- **Hub vs Switch**: Switch is intelligent (packet filtering); Hub is not (broadcasts).
- **Repeater**: Boosts signal for long distances.
- **Gateway**: Connects dissimilar networks.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Hub vs Switch**: Switch is faster and more secure as it sends data only to the destination MAC address.
- **Fiber Optic Advantage**: Immune to electromagnetic interference.
- **Star Topology Fault**: If the central hub fails, the whole network goes down.
