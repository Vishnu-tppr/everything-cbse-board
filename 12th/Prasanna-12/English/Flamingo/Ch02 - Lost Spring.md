# 📖 Ch02 - Lost Spring

> **English** | **Author**: Anees Jung | [[📖 English]]
> This story explores the "stolen childhood" of children forced into labor by poverty and tradition. It focuses on the lives of rag-pickers in Seemapuri and bangle-makers in Firozabad.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Saheb-e-Alam (Seemapuri)
- **Background**: Refugees from Bangladesh (1971); live in structures of mud, with roofs of tin and tarpaulin; no sewage, no drainage, no running water.
- **Garbage as Gold**: To children, garbage is wrapped in wonder (they might find a rupee or a ten-rupee note); to adults, it is a means of survival.
- **The Tea Stall**: Saheb gets a job at a tea stall but loses his freedom ("Saheb is no longer his own master").

### 2. Mukesh (Firozabad)
- **Background**: Family of bangle-makers; live in dingy cells without air and light; high temperature near glass furnaces.
- **The Stigma of Caste**: Born in the caste of bangle-makers, they are destined to continue the trade.
- **Mukesh's Dream**: Wants to be a **motor mechanic**. He is determined to break the vicious circle.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Themes: Poverty and Social Injustice
- The "Vicious Circle" of Sahukars, middlemen, policemen, keepers of law, bureaucrats, and politicians.
- **Irony of the Name**: "Saheb-e-Alam" means "Lord of the Universe," but he is a barefoot rag-picker.

### ⚡ Contrast
- Seemapuri is on the periphery of Delhi yet "miles away from it, metaphorically" (lack of development).
- Saheb's loss of freedom vs Mukesh's spark of rebellion.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Mukesh's Grandmother's Attitude**: She believes it is their "karam" or destiny. This shows the deep-rooted acceptance of poverty.
- **Hazardous Conditions**: Mention the high risk of losing eyesight in the glass industry due to dust from polishing glass.
- **Seemapuri**: It is not just a slum; it is a settlement of 10,000 rag-pickers without identity except ration cards for grain.
