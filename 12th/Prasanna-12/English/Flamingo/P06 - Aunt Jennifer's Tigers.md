# 🎨 P06 - Aunt Jennifer's Tigers

> **English** | **Author**: Adrienne Rich | [[📖 English]]
> A poem addressing the constraints of married life for women and the use of art as a medium of self-expression and escape.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. The Tigers (The Art)
- **Description**: "Bright topaz denizens of a world of green." They are "chivalric" and do not fear the men beneath the tree.
- **Pace**: They "prance" and "sleek" in certain certainty.

### 2. Aunt Jennifer (The Artist)
- **Struggle**: Her fingers "flutter" through her wool. She finds even the ivory needle hard to pull.
- **The Burden**: "The massive weight of Uncle's wedding band" sits heavily upon her hand.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Themes: Patriarchal Oppression and Artistic Legacy
- The contrast between the weak, terrified woman and the strong, fearless tigers she creates.
- **Irony**: Even in death, she will be "ringed with ordeals she was mastered by," while her tigers will go on prancing.

### ⚡ Poetic Devices
- **Metaphor**: "Bright topaz denizens."
- **Symbolism**: "Wedding band" (Symbol of bondage/oppression).
- **Alliteration**: "prancing, proud," "fingers fluttering."
- **Contrast**: The vibrant, fearless tigers vs the frail, terrified woman.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Uncle's Wedding Band**: It is not "heavy" in physical weight, but in psychological burden and social constraint.
- **Aunt Jennifer's Death**: Even after she dies, she remains "mastered" by her ordeals, but her creation (the tigers) lives on as a testament to her suppressed desire for freedom.
- **Chivalric**: Refers to the tigers' noble, brave, and respectful manner, which is the opposite of the oppression she faces.
