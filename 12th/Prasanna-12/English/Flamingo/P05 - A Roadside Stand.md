# 🎨 P05 - A Roadside Stand

> **English** | **Author**: Robert Frost | [[📖 English]]
> A poem depicting the economic divide between the rural poor and the urban rich, focusing on a humble roadside stand and its owners' unfulfilled hopes.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. The Plea of the Rural Poor
- The stand was put up to earn "some city money." It's not a plea for bread, but for the "cash" that supports the city's growth.
- **The Attitude of the Rich**: They are "polished" and move in their cars, complaining that the stand has "marred the landscape."

### 2. The False Promises
- "Greedy good-doers" and "beneficent beasts of prey" promise a better life to the villagers only to exploit them and rob them of their "sleep."

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Themes: Rural-Urban Divide and Hypocrisy
- The city people only stop for directions, a U-turn, or to ask for "a gallon of gas" (which the villagers don't have).
- **The Poet's Pain**: He feels a "childish longing in vain" for the villagers' hope to be realized.

### ⚡ Poetic Devices
- **Oxymoron**: "Greedy good-doers," "beneficent beasts of prey."
- **Personification**: "The sadness that lurks near the open window."
- **Metaphor**: "Trusting sorrow" (The villagers' trust in the city folk which only brings sorrow).

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Why they built the stand**: Not to sell exotic things, but to have a connection to the city's wealth.
- **The "Gallon of Gas"**: Shows the disconnect—the city folk don't see the villagers' poverty, they only see their own needs.
- **The "Squeal of Brakes"**: Represents the fleeting hope of the villagers whenever a car slows down.
