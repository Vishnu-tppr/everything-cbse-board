# 📖 Ch03 - Deep Water

> **English** | **Author**: William Douglas | [[📖 English]]
> An autobiographical account of overcoming the "fear of water" through determination and perseverance.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. The Childhood Aversion
- **Age 3 or 4**: Knocked down by waves at California beach with his father.
- **Y.M.C.A. Pool Incident**: A "big bruiser of a boy" (18 years old) tossed 10-year-old Douglas into the deep end.

### 2. The Near-Death Experience
- **Strategy**: "When I hit the bottom, I would make a big jump, come to the surface, lie flat on it, and paddle to the edge of the pool."
- **Reality**: He went down three times, experienced "stark terror," and finally blacked out ("I crossed to oblivion").

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Theme: Conquest of Fear
- **The Instructor**: Taught him piece by piece (Belt $\to$ Rope $\to$ Pulley). Integrated the pieces into a whole swimmer.
- **Key Quote**: "All we have to fear is fear itself" (Franklin D. Roosevelt).

### ⚡ Symbolism
- **Water**: Represents the paralyzing power of fear.
- **The Jump**: Represents the initial, unsuccessful attempt to use logic against raw terror.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **The Instructor's Role**: The instructor did not just teach him how to swim; he "built a swimmer" out of Douglas in seven months.
- **Final Test**: Douglas wasn't satisfied with the pool; he went to Lake Wentworth and Warm Lake to ensure the "tiny vestiges of fear" were gone.
- **The "Bruiser"**: He did it "just for fun," highlighting the careless cruelty that can cause lifelong trauma.
