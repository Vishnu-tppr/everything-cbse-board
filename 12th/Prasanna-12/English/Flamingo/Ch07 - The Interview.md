# 📖 Ch07 - The Interview

> **English** | **Author**: Christopher Silvester | [[📖 English]]
> This chapter explores the nature of interviews as a medium of communication and the varying perspectives of celebrities. It includes an excerpt from Umberto Eco’s interview.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Perspectives on Interviews
- **Positive**: A source of truth; a supreme medium of communication.
- **Negative**: "A thumbprint on the windpipe" (H.G. Wells); an unwarranted intrusion into personal life (V.S. Naipaul).

### 2. Umberto Eco
- A professor at the University of Bologna. He writes novels on Sundays.
- **Interstices**: Eco believes that if we eliminate the "empty spaces" (interstices) in the universe, it will shrink. He uses these empty spaces in time to write.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Theme: The Art of the Novel
- **The Name of the Rose**: A multi-layered novel (Metaphysics, Theology, Medieval History).
- **The Secret of Success**: Eco says the success is a "mystery." If he had written it ten years earlier or later, it might not have been a hit.

### ⚡ Interviewer: Mukund Padmanabhan
- The interview shows Eco as a man who doesn't take himself too seriously despite being a world-renowned scholar.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Umberto Eco's Identity**: He is a **Scholar first**, and a novelist second. He participates in academic conferences, not pen-club meetings.
- **Interstices Concept**: It means the "empty spaces" in our schedule (waiting for an elevator, etc.).
- **Celebrity Aversion**: Note why authors like Rudyard Kipling or Lewis Carroll disliked being interviewed (immoral, a crime, soul-stealing).
