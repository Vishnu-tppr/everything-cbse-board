# 📖 Ch05 - Indigo

> **English** | **Author**: Louis Fischer | [[📖 English]]
> An account of Gandhi's struggle for the sharecroppers of Champaran. It marks the beginning of the end of the British rule in India.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. The Conflict
- **Sharecropping System**: Peasants forced to grow Indigo on 15% of land and surrender the entire crop as rent.
- **Synthetic Indigo**: Germany developed it; landlords wanted "compensation" to release peasants from the 15% agreement.

### 2. Rajkumar Shukla
- Described as "resolute." He followed Gandhi everywhere (Lucknow, Cawnpore, Ahmedabad) until Gandhi agreed to visit Champaran.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Theme: Civil Disobedience
- **Motihari Court**: Thousands of peasants gathered around the courthouse. The British felt powerless.
- **Key Moment**: Gandhi pleaded guilty but explained he was in a "conflict of duties"—between law and human service.

### ⚡ Social Reform
- Gandhi didn't just fight for the 25% refund. He worked on health (Castor oil, Quinine, Sulphur ointment) and education (opening schools with Kasturba).

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **The Refund**: The amount (25%) was less important than the fact that the landlords were forced to surrender part of their "prestige."
- **Charles Freer Andrews**: Gandhi refused his help because it showed "weakness of heart." He wanted Indians to be self-reliant.
- **The Official Inquiry**: Gandhi presented a mountain of evidence; the landlords agreed in principle to the refund.
