# 📖 V08 - Memories of Childhood

> **English** | **Authors**: Zitkala-Sa & Bama | [[📖 English]]
> Two autobiographical accounts of social discrimination—one based on race (Native American) and the other on caste (Dalit).

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. The Cutting of my Long Hair (Zitkala-Sa)
- **The Incident**: Native American girl forced to cut her long hair at a boarding school. 
- **Symbolism**: In her culture, short hair was for cowards or those in mourning.
- **Resistance**: She hid under a bed but was eventually dragged out and shorn. "Then I lost my spirit."

### 2. We Too are Human Beings (Bama)
- **The Incident**: A young Dalit girl sees an elder from her community carrying food by a string so as not to "pollute" it for the landlord.
- **The Lesson**: Her brother Annan tells her that because they are born into a low caste, they are never given honor. He tells her that **Education** is the only way to win respect.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Themes: Identity and Education
- Both narrators experience a loss of dignity at a young age.
- **Bama's Success**: She took her brother's advice to heart, studied hard, and many people became her friends.

### ⚡ Contrast
- Zitkala-Sa’s struggle is physical and cultural (hair).
- Bama’s struggle is social and systemic (untouchability).

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Judewin’s Warning**: Judewin (a friend) told Zitkala-Sa to "submit," but Zitkala-Sa decided to "rebel."
- **The Elder's Behavior**: Bama initially thought the elder's way of carrying the packet was "funny" until she understood the grim reality of untouchability.
- **The Advice**: Annan’s words are crucial: "If you study and make progress, you can throw away these indignities."
