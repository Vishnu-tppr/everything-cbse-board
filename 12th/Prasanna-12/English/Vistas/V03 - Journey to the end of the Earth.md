# 📖 V03 - Journey to the end of the Earth

> **English** | **Author**: Tishani Doshi | [[📖 English]]
> A travelogue exploring Antarctica's significance in understanding Earth's past, present, and future, through the 'Students on Ice' program.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Antarctica: The Time Machine
- 90% of the Earth's total ice volumes are stored here.
- No human markers, no trees, no billboards, no buildings. It gives a sense of "geological time."
- **Gondwana**: The ancient supercontinent that Antarctica was once a part of, 650 million years ago.

### 2. Human Impact
- Humans have been around for only 12,000 years (a "fraction of a second" in geological time).
- Rapid population growth and use of fossil fuels have created a "blanket of carbon dioxide," leading to global warming.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Theme: Environmental Awareness
- **Students on Ice**: Program led by Geoff Green. Aims to take high school students to the ends of the world to foster respect for the planet.
- **Phytoplankton**: Microscopic grasses of the sea. They are the base of the food chain. Any change in the ozone layer affects them, which affects the entire global carbon cycle.

### ⚡ Observation
- "Take care of the small things and the big things will take care of themselves." (e.g., Phytoplankton).

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Ozone Depletion**: Mention the link between ozone layer depletion and its effect on phytoplankton.
- **Ice Core Secrets**: Antarctica holds half-million-year-old carbon records trapped in its layers of ice.
- **Geoff Green's Logic**: He stopped taking celebrities and the "retired rich" because they only "gave back" in a limited way; students are the "future policy-makers."
