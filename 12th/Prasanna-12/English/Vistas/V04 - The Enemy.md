# 📖 V04 - The Enemy

> **English** | **Author**: Pearl S. Buck | [[📖 English]]
> A story set during WWII, exploring the conflict between national loyalty and humanitarian duty through the character of Dr. Sadao Hoki.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. The Dilemma
- Dr. Sadao finds an American prisoner of war (POW) washed ashore near his house in Japan.
- **Conflict**: As a Japanese citizen, he should hand him over. As a doctor, he must save the dying man.

### 2. The Decision
- Sadao and his wife Hana decide to take him in and operate.
- **Hana’s Role**: She washes the man herself when the servants refuse and assists Sadao in the operating room despite her nausea.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Theme: Humanity above Nationality
- Sadao's professionalism: He ignores the man's identity during surgery ("My friend," he says to the patient).
- **The General**: A selfish man who neglects his duty to report the POW because he needs Sadao for his own health. He promises assassins but "forgets."

### ⚡ The Escape
- Sadao helps the man (Tom) escape to a nearby island with food, clothes, and a flashlight, proving that human compassion transcends borders.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **The Servants**: They represent the common, narrow-minded patriotic view. They leave the house because they feel Sadao is a traitor.
- **Sadao’s Father**: He was a traditionalist whose only concern was Sadao’s education and Japan’s future. Sadao’s house was built with Japanese materials for him.
- **The Flashlight Signal**: One flash if food ran out but he was still there; no flash meant he had escaped safely.
