# 📖 V06 - On the Face of It

> **English** | **Author**: Susan Hill | [[📖 English]]
> A play exploring the psychological impact of physical disability and the transformative power of companionship and a positive outlook.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. The Characters
- **Derry**: A 14-year-old boy with a face burnt by acid. He is withdrawn, bitter, and expects people to be afraid of him.
- **Mr. Lamb**: An elderly man with a tin leg. He is optimistic, loves his garden, and keeps his house doors open.

### 2. The Interaction
- Derry enters Mr. Lamb’s garden thinking it’s empty. Mr. Lamb welcomes him and challenges his self-pity.
- **Lamb's Philosophy**: "Everything’s the same, but everything is different." He compares weeds to flowers, both being "growing things."

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Theme: Overcoming Alienation
- Derry’s mother is overprotective and reinforces his fear. Mr. Lamb encourages him to "stand on his own feet" and look at the world.
- **The Tragedy**: When Derry finally returns to the garden with a new spirit, he finds Mr. Lamb has fallen from his ladder and died.

### ⚡ Symbolism
- **The Garden**: A place of growth and acceptance.
- **The Tin Leg vs Burnt Face**: Both have physical scars, but Lamb chooses to live fully while Derry chooses to hide.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **The "Ugly" Face**: Derry says, "I'm not afraid of you... they are afraid of me." He thinks his face is the most "terrible thing."
- **Lamb's Loneliness**: Despite his optimism, Lamb is lonely ("I’m not a hero... I’m just a man with a tin leg"). His death is more tragic because he had just started to change a young boy's life.
- **Bees**: Mr. Lamb hears them "singing," not "buzzing." It’s all about perspective.
