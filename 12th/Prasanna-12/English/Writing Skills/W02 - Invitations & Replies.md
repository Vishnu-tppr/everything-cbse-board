# 📝 W02 - Invitations & Replies

> **English** | **Marks**: 4 | [[📖 English]]
> Invitations can be **Formal** (Card or Letter) or **Informal**. Replies must acknowledge the invitation and express acceptance or regret.

---

## 🎯 Formal Invitation (Card Format)
- **Who**: The host's name (e.g., Mrs. & Mr. Sharma).
- **Request**: Standard phrase ("Request the pleasure of your company...").
- **What**: The occasion (e.g., Marriage of their daughter).
- **When**: Date, Time, Venue.
- **RSVP**: Left bottom corner with contact details.

> **⚠️ Note**: Used for large gatherings. Always 3rd person. No box required but looks better in one.

---

## 📐 Formal Reply (Acceptance/Refusal)
- **Acceptance**: "Mrs. & Mr. Verma thank Mrs. & Mr. Sharma for the kind invitation... and are delighted to accept the same."
- **Refusal**: "...thank for the kind invitation... but regret their inability to attend due to a prior engagement."

---

## ⚡ Informal Invitation & Reply
- Follows the format of a personal letter.
- Uses 1st and 2nd person ("I", "You").
- Tone is casual and warm.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Person Confusion**: Formal = 3rd Person (*They*). Informal = 1st/2nd Person (*I/You*).
- **The "Prior Engagement"**: In formal refusals, always use this phrase or "unavoidable circumstances."
- **Abbreviations**: Avoid abbreviations in formal invitations (Write "Twenty-second" instead of "22nd").
