# ⚡ Ch06 - Electromagnetic Induction

> **Physics** | **Marks**: ~6-8 | [[🔬 Physics]]
> This chapter covers how changing magnetic fields induce electric current, focusing on Faraday's Laws, Lenz's Law, and the concept of Self/Mutual Induction.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Magnetic Flux ($\phi_B$)
- $\phi = \vec{B} \cdot \vec{A} = BA \cos \theta$.
- Changing $B, A$, or $\theta$ induces an EMF.

### 2. Faraday’s Laws of EMI
- **First Law**: Whenever magnetic flux through a circuit changes, an EMF is induced.
- **Second Law**: The magnitude of induced EMF is equal to the rate of change of magnetic flux. $\epsilon = - \frac{d\phi}{dt}$.

### 3. Lenz’s Law
- The direction of induced current is such that it opposes the change that produced it.
- **Conservation of Energy**: Lenz’s law is a consequence of the law of conservation of energy.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### ⚡ Mandatory Derivations
1. **Motional EMF**: $\epsilon = Blv$. (Moving a conductor in a magnetic field).
2. **Self-Inductance of a Solenoid**: $L = \mu_0 n^2 l A$. (Stored energy $U = \frac{1}{2}LI^2$).
3. **Mutual Inductance of two Coaxial Solenoids**: $M = \mu_0 n_1 n_2 l A$.

### 🧪 Eddy Currents
- Circulating currents induced in bulk pieces of conductors when magnetic flux changes.
- **Applications**: Magnetic braking, Induction furnace, Electromagnetic damping.
- **Minimization**: Using laminated cores.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Lenz’s Law Direction**: Often confused with the external field direction. Remember: the induced field opposes the **change** in external flux.
- **Self vs Mutual**: Self-induction involves a single coil; Mutual induction involves the coupling between two separate coils.
- **Units**: Inductance is measured in **Henry (H)**. $1 H = 1 Wb/A$.
