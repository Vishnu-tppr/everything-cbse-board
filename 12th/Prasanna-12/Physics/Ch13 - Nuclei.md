# 🧬 Ch13 - Nuclei

> **Physics** | **Marks**: ~4 | [[🔬 Physics]]
> This chapter covers the composition and properties of the nucleus, nuclear forces, and nuclear energy (Fission & Fusion).

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Composition of Nucleus
- **Isotopes**: Same $Z$, different $A$ (e.g., $^1_1H, ^2_1H, ^3_1H$).
- **Isobars**: Same $A$, different $Z$.
- **Isotones**: Same number of neutrons ($A-Z$).
- **Size**: $R = R_0 A^{1/3}$, where $R_0 = 1.2 \times 10^{-15} m$.
- **Density**: Nuclear density is constant and independent of $A$. ($\sim 2.3 \times 10^{17} kg/m^3$).

### 2. Mass-Energy Equivalence
- **Einstein’s Relation**: $E = mc^2$.
- **Mass Defect** ($\Delta M$): Difference between mass of nucleus and sum of its constituents. $\Delta M = [Z m_p + (A-Z) m_n] - M_{nucleus}$.
- **Binding Energy** ($BE$): Energy required to break the nucleus. $BE = \Delta M \times 931.5 MeV$ (if $\Delta M$ is in $amu$).

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Binding Energy Curve
- Plot of $BE$ per nucleon ($BE/A$) vs $A$.
- **Insights**:
  - Stable nuclei have high $BE/A$ ($\sim 8 MeV$). Peak at $^{56}Fe$.
  - Low $A$ nuclei can undergo **Fusion** to increase $BE/A$.
  - High $A$ nuclei can undergo **Fission** to increase $BE/A$.

### ⚡ Nuclear Force
- Strongest force in nature.
- **Short-range** ($\sim$ few femtometers).
- **Charge-independent** (P-P force $\approx$ P-N force).
- **Saturation property**.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Density Paradox**: Heavy nuclei are larger, but their **density is the same** as light nuclei.
- **$amu$ to $MeV$**: 1 amu = $1.66 \times 10^{-27} kg = 931.5 MeV$. Don't use $c^2$ in numericals if $amu$ is given; just multiply by 931.5.
- **Stable vs Unstable**: If $BE/A$ is low, the nucleus is unstable.
