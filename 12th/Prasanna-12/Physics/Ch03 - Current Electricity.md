# ⚡ Ch03 - Current Electricity

> **Physics** | **Marks**: ~8 | [[🔬 Physics]]
> This chapter shifts from static to moving charges, introducing Ohm's Law, Drift Velocity, and complex Circuit Analysis using Kirchhoff's Laws.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Electric Current ($I$)
- Rate of flow of charge: $I = \frac{dq}{dt}$. (SI Unit: Ampere).
- **Current Density** ($J$): $J = I/A$. (Vector quantity).

### 2. Ohm's Law
- $V = IR$ (where $R$ is resistance).
- **Resistivity** ($\rho$): $R = \rho \frac{l}{A}$.
- **Conductivity** ($\sigma$): $\sigma = 1/\rho$.
- Vector form: $\vec{J} = \sigma \vec{E}$.

### 3. Drift Velocity ($v_d$)
- The average velocity with which free electrons get drifted towards the positive terminal: $v_d = \frac{eE\tau}{m}$.
- Relation with current: $I = n e A v_d$.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### ⚡ Mandatory Derivations
1. **Relation between $I$ and $v_d$**: $I = n e A v_d$.
2. **Ohm's Law from first principles**: $R = \frac{ml}{ne^2\tau A}$.
3. **Internal Resistance of a Cell**: $r = R \left( \frac{E}{V} - 1 \right)$.

### 🧪 Circuit Analysis
- **Kirchhoff's First Law (KCL)**: Junction rule. $\sum I_{in} = \sum I_{out}$. (Based on **Conservation of Charge**).
- **Kirchhoff's Second Law (KVL)**: Loop rule. $\sum \Delta V = 0$. (Based on **Conservation of Energy**).
- **Wheatstone Bridge**: Balanced condition $\frac{P}{Q} = \frac{R}{S}$.

### 🔋 Cells & EMF
- **EMF ($E$) vs Terminal Voltage ($V$)**: $V = E - Ir$.
- **Cells in Series**: $E_{eq} = E_1 + E_2$, $r_{eq} = r_1 + r_2$.
- **Cells in Parallel**: $E_{eq} = \frac{E_1 r_2 + E_2 r_1}{r_1 + r_2}$.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Temperature Effect**: Resistance of metals **increases** with temperature; for semiconductors, it **decreases**.
- **Loop Sign Convention**: Be careful with $\pm$ signs in KVL. Consistency is key.
- **Drift speed magnitude**: It is extremely slow ($\sim 10^{-4} m/s$), yet current is established instantly. Explain via electromagnetic wave propagation.
