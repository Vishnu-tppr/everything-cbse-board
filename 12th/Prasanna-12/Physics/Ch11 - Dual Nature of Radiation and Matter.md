# 💡 Ch11 - Dual Nature of Radiation and Matter

> **Physics** | **Marks**: ~4 | [[🔬 Physics]]
> This chapter covers the particle nature of light (Photoelectric Effect) and the wave nature of matter (de-Broglie hypothesis).

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Photoelectric Effect
- Emission of electrons from a metal surface when light of suitable frequency falls on it.
- **Threshold Frequency** ($\nu_0$): Minimum frequency required for emission.
- **Stopping Potential** ($V_0$): Negative potential at which photocurrent becomes zero. It depends on frequency, NOT intensity.
- **Photon Properties**: Energy $E = h\nu$, Momentum $p = h/\lambda$.

### 2. Einstein’s Photoelectric Equation
- $K_{max} = h\nu - \Phi_0 = eV_0$.
- Where $\Phi_0 = h\nu_0$ is the **Work Function**.
- Explains: Why there's no time lag; why $K_{max}$ depends only on frequency.

### 3. Wave Nature of Matter
- **de-Broglie Hypothesis**: $\lambda = h/p = \frac{h}{mv}$.
- For an electron accelerated by $V$ volts: $\lambda = \frac{h}{\sqrt{2mqV}} \approx \frac{12.27}{\sqrt{V}} \text{ \AA}$.

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Intensity vs Frequency**: Intensity increases the *number* of photoelectrons; Frequency increases the *kinetic energy* of photoelectrons.
- **Unit Conversions**: Energy is often in $eV$. Remember $1 eV = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} J$.
- **Photon Mass**: A photon has zero rest mass but finite momentum.
