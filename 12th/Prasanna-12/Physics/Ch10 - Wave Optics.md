# 🌊 Ch10 - Wave Optics

> **Physics** | **Marks**: ~6-8 | [[🔬 Physics]]
> This chapter treats light as a wave, introducing Huygens' Principle, the phenomena of Interference and Diffraction.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Huygens’ Principle
- Each point on a **wavefront** acts as a source of secondary wavelets.
- Wavefront: Surface of constant phase.
- **Ray** is always perpendicular to the wavefront.

### 2. Interference of Light
- Superposition of two coherent waves results in redistribution of energy.
- **Coherent Sources**: Constant phase difference over time.
- **Conditions for Constructive (Bright)**: $\Delta \phi = 2n\pi$ or path difference $\Delta x = n\lambda$.
- **Conditions for Destructive (Dark)**: $\Delta \phi = (2n+1)\pi$ or path difference $\Delta x = (n+1/2)\lambda$.

### 3. Young’s Double Slit Experiment (YDSE)
- Used to prove the wave nature of light.
- **Fringe Width** ($\beta$): $\beta = \frac{\lambda D}{d}$.
- All fringes (bright and dark) are of equal width.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### ⚡ Mandatory Derivations
1. **Proof of Reflection/Refraction** using Huygens’ Principle.
2. **Expression for Fringe Width** in YDSE.
3. **Condition for Interference Maxima/Minima**.

### 🧪 Diffraction
- Bending of light around the corners of an obstacle.
- **Single Slit Diffraction**:
  - Central Maxima width = $2\lambda D / d$.
  - Intensity decreases rapidly for secondary maxima.
- **Difference from Interference**: Interference is superposition of waves from two different sources; Diffraction is from different parts of the same wavefront.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Phase vs Path Difference**: $\Delta \phi = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda} \Delta x$. Don't swap them in formulas.
- **Angular Fringe Width**: $\theta = \lambda / d$. It does NOT depend on $D$.
- **Effect of Medium**: When YDSE is immersed in water, $\lambda$ decreases ($\lambda' = \lambda/n$), so fringe width $\beta$ decreases.
