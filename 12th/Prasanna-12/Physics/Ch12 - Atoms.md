# ⚛️ Ch12 - Atoms

> **Physics** | **Marks**: ~4 | [[🔬 Physics]]
> This chapter covers the structure of the atom, focusing on Rutherford's alpha-scattering experiment and Bohr's Model of the Hydrogen Atom.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Alpha-Particle Scattering (Rutherford)
- Most $\alpha$-particles pass undeflected $\implies$ atom is mostly empty space.
- Small fraction deflected $\implies$ positive charge is concentrated in a tiny **Nucleus**.
- **Distance of Closest Approach** ($r_0$): $r_0 = \frac{1}{4\pi\epsilon_0} \frac{2Ze^2}{K}$.

### 2. Bohr’s Model (Hydrogen)
- **Postulate 1**: Electrons move in stable orbits without radiating energy.
- **Postulate 2 (Quantization)**: $L = mvr = \frac{nh}{2\pi}$.
- **Postulate 3**: Energy emitted/absorbed only during transitions: $h\nu = E_i - E_f$.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### ⚡ Mandatory Formulas
1. **Radius of $n^{th}$ orbit**: $r_n = \frac{n^2 h^2 \epsilon_0}{\pi m e^2} \approx 0.53 n^2 \text{ \AA}$. ($r_n \propto n^2$).
2. **Energy of $n^{th}$ orbit**: $E_n = - \frac{me^4}{8\epsilon_0^2 h^2 n^2} \approx - \frac{13.6}{n^2} eV$.
3. **Spectral Series**: $\frac{1}{\lambda} = R \left( \frac{1}{n_f^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2} \right)$.
   - Lyman: $n_f=1$ (UV).
   - Balmer: $n_f=2$ (Visible).
   - Paschen/Brackett/Pfund: $n_f=3,4,5$ (Infrared).

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Kinetic vs Potential Energy**: $K = |E|$, $U = 2E$. Total energy $E$ is **Negative**.
- **Ionization vs Excitation**: Ionization is removing to $n=\infty$; Excitation is moving to a higher $n$.
- **Shortest vs Longest $\lambda$**: 
  - Shortest $\lambda \implies$ Max energy ($\infty \to n$).
  - Longest $\lambda \implies$ Min energy ($n+1 \to n$).
