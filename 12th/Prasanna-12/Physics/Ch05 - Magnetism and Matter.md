# 🧲 Ch05 - Magnetism and Matter

> **Physics** | **Marks**: ~3-4 | [[🔬 Physics]]
> This chapter focuses on the properties of permanent magnets, the Earth's magnetic field, and the magnetic properties of materials.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Bar Magnet
- Equivalent to a solenoid. 
- **Magnetic Dipole Moment** ($m$): $m = q_m \times 2l$ (Pole strength $\times$ length).
- **Magnetic Field Lines**: Form continuous closed loops (unlike electric field lines). They go from N to S outside and S to N inside.

### 2. Earth’s Magnetism
- **Magnetic Declination** ($\alpha$): Angle between magnetic meridian and geographic meridian.
- **Magnetic Inclination/Dip** ($\delta$): Angle that the total magnetic field of earth makes with the horizontal surface.
  - At **Equator**: $\delta = 0^\circ$.
  - At **Poles**: $\delta = 90^\circ$.
- **Horizontal Component** ($B_H$): $B_H = B \cos \delta$.

### 3. Magnetic Properties of Materials
| Property | Diamagnetic | Paramagnetic | Ferromagnetic |
|----------|-------------|--------------|---------------|
| **Behavior in Field** | Repelled weakly | Attracted weakly | Attracted strongly |
| **Susceptibility ($\chi$)** | Negative (Small) | Positive (Small) | Positive (Large) |
| **Permeability ($\mu_r$)** | $\mu_r < 1$ | $\mu_r > 1$ | $\mu_r \gg 1$ |
| **Examples** | Water, Copper, Bi | Al, Na, Oxygen | Iron, Cobalt, Ni |

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Key Concepts
- **Curie’s Law**: Susceptibility of paramagnetic materials is inversely proportional to absolute temperature: $\chi = C/T$.
- **Curie Temperature**: The temperature above which a ferromagnetic material becomes paramagnetic.
- **Magnetic Flux ($\phi_B$)**: $\phi = \vec{B} \cdot \vec{A} = BA \cos \theta$. Gauss’s Law for magnetism states $\oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{A} = 0$ (Monopoles do not exist).

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Monopoles**: Never assume a single magnetic pole exists. Breaking a magnet creates two new dipoles.
- **Dip at Equator/Poles**: This is a high-frequency MCQ. **Equator = 0, Pole = 90**.
- **$\chi$ vs $\mu_r$**: Remember the relation $\mu_r = 1 + \chi$.
