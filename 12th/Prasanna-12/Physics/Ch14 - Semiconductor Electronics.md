# 🔌 Ch14 - Semiconductor Electronics

> **Physics** | **Marks**: ~7 | [[🔬 Physics]]
> This chapter covers the physics of semiconductors, the P-N junction, and electronic devices like Diodes and Rectifiers.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Classification of Solids
- **Metals**: Overlapping bands or partially filled conduction band.
- **Insulators**: Large energy gap ($E_g > 3 eV$).
- **Semiconductors**: Small energy gap ($E_g < 3 eV$).
  - **Intrinsic**: Pure semiconductor (e.g., Si, Ge). $n_e = n_h = n_i$.
  - **Extrinsic**: Doped. 
    - **n-type**: Doped with Pentavalent (P, As). $n_e \gg n_h$.
    - **p-type**: Doped with Trivalent (B, Al). $n_h \gg n_e$.

### 2. P-N Junction
- **Formation**: Diffusion and Drift of charges creates a **Depletion Region** and **Barrier Potential**.
- **Biasing**:
  - **Forward Bias**: P to +ve, N to -ve. Depletion layer shrinks. Current flows.
  - **Reverse Bias**: P to -ve, N to +ve. Depletion layer thickens. No current flows (except micro-amps).

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Key Devices
- **p-n Junction Diode as Rectifier**:
  - **Half-wave**: One diode. Output is half of input AC.
  - **Full-wave**: Two diodes + Center-tap transformer. Output is full rectified AC.
- **Special Purpose Diodes**:
  - **LED**: Forward biased; emits light on recombination.
  - **Photodiode**: Reverse biased; current changes with light intensity.
  - **Solar Cell**: No bias; generates EMF from sunlight.

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Holes vs Electrons**: Remember that holes are *effective* charge carriers representing vacant electron sites.
- **Reverse Saturation Current**: It is independent of voltage but highly dependent on **temperature**.
- **Depletion Layer Thickness**: Shrinks in Forward, Expands in Reverse. Don't swap this in conceptual answers.
