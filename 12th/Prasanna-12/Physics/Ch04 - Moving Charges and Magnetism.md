# 🧲 Ch04 - Moving Charges and Magnetism

> **Physics** | **Marks**: ~8 | [[🔬 Physics]]
> This chapter introduces the magnetic effects of electric current, Biot-Savart Law, Ampere's Circuital Law, and the force on moving charges.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Magnetic Force ($\vec{F}_m$)
- On a charge $q$: $\vec{F} = q(\vec{v} \times \vec{B}) \implies F = qvB \sin \theta$.
- **Lorentz Force**: Total force $\vec{F} = q[\vec{E} + (\vec{v} \times \vec{B})]$.
- On a current-carrying wire: $\vec{F} = I(\vec{l} \times \vec{B})$.

### 2. Biot-Savart Law
- Magnetic field due to a small element $dl$: $d\vec{B} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \frac{I d\vec{l} \times \hat{r}}{r^2}$.
- Field at center of circular loop: $B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2R}$.

### 3. Ampere’s Circuital Law (ACL)
- $\oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{l} = \mu_0 I_{enclosed}$.
- Field inside a **Solenoid**: $B = \mu_0 n I$ (where $n = N/l$).
- Field of a **Toroid**: $B = \mu_0 n I$.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### ⚡ Mandatory Derivations
1. **Field on axis of circular loop**: $B = \frac{\mu_0 I R^2}{2(R^2+x^2)^{3/2}}$.
2. **Force between two parallel currents**: $\frac{F}{l} = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2}{2\pi d}$. (Attractive if parallel, Repulsive if anti-parallel).
3. **Torque on current loop**: $\vec{\tau} = \vec{m} \times \vec{B}$ (where $\vec{m} = NI\vec{A}$).

### 🔧 Devices
- **Moving Coil Galvanometer (MCG)**: 
  - Principle: $NIAB = k\phi$.
  - **Current Sensitivity**: $S_i = \frac{\phi}{I} = \frac{NBA}{k}$.
  - **Voltage Sensitivity**: $S_v = \frac{\phi}{V} = \frac{NBA}{kR}$.
- **Conversion**:
  - To **Ammeter**: Connect low resistance ($S$) in parallel. $S = \frac{I_g G}{I - I_g}$.
  - To **Voltmeter**: Connect high resistance ($R$) in series. $R = \frac{V}{I_g} - G$.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Work Done by Magnetic Field**: Since $\vec{F}_m \perp \vec{v}$, the work done is **Always Zero**. Kinetic energy is constant; only direction changes.
- **Magnetic Moment Direction**: Use Right-Hand Thumb Rule. It points from South to North of the equivalent magnet.
- **Conversion Logic**: Forgetting that $G$ (galvanometer resistance) must be included in total resistance calculations.
