# 🧬 Ch10 - Biomolecules

> **Chemistry** | **Marks**: ~7 | [[🧪 Chemistry]]
> This chapter covers the chemical nature and functions of essential biological molecules like Carbohydrates, Proteins, and Nucleic Acids.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Carbohydrates
- **Classification**: Monosaccharides (Glucose, Fructose), Oligosaccharides (Sucrose, Maltose, Lactose), Polysaccharides (Starch, Cellulose, Glycogen).
- **Glucose**:
  - Open chain structure vs Cyclic (Pyranose).
  - Anomers: $\alpha$ and $\beta$ glucose (differ at $C-1$).
- **Reducing Sugars**: All monosaccharides and some disaccharides (Maltose, Lactose). Sucrose is **Non-reducing**.

### 2. Proteins
- **Amino Acids**: Building blocks. All are $\alpha$-amino acids.
  - **Zwitterion**: Dipolar ion form ($NH_3^+$ and $COO^-$).
  - **Essential vs Non-essential**: 10 are essential (must be in diet).
- **Structure**: Primary, Secondary ($\alpha$-helix, $\beta$-pleated), Tertiary, Quaternary.
- **Denaturation**: Loss of biological activity due to change in pH or Temp. (Primary structure remains intact).

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Nucleic Acids
- **Components**: Pentose Sugar + Nitrogenous Base + Phosphoric Acid.
- **DNA**: Deoxyribose; Bases: A, G, C, T. (Double helix).
- **RNA**: Ribose; Bases: A, G, C, U. (Single strand).
- **Nucleoside** vs **Nucleotide**: Nucleotide = Nucleoside + Phosphate.

### ⚡ Vitamins & Enzymes
- **Water Soluble**: B and C. (Cannot be stored).
- **Fat Soluble**: A, D, E, K. (Stored in liver/adipose tissue).
- **Enzymes**: Biocatalysts; specific in action.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Sucrose Linkage**: It is an $\alpha-D-glucose$ and $\beta-D-fructose$ linkage ($C1-C2$). Since both reducing groups are bonded, it is non-reducing.
- **DNA Bases**: U is ONLY in RNA; T is ONLY in DNA.
- **Denaturation**: Always mention that **Primary structure** (sequence of amino acids) is NOT affected.
