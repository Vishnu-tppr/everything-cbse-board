# 🍍 Ch08 - Aldehydes, Ketones and Carboxylic Acids

> **Chemistry** | **Marks**: ~8-10 | [[🧪 Chemistry]]
> This chapter focuses on the Carbonyl group ($C=O$), covering the preparation and highly diverse reactivity of Aldehydes, Ketones, and Carboxylic Acids.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Preparation Methods
- **Aldehydes**: Rosenmund Reduction ($RCOCl \to RCHO$), Stephen Reaction ($RCN \to RCHO$), Etard Reaction (Toluene $\to$ Benzaldehyde).
- **Ketones**: From Acyl chlorides with dialkyl cadmium, or Friedel-Crafts Acylation.

### 2. Nucleophilic Addition Reactions
- Carbonyl carbon is electrophilic.
- **Reactions with**: $HCN$ (Cyanohydrin), $NaHSO_3$ (Bisulphite adduct), Grignard ($1^\circ/2^\circ/3^\circ$ alcohols), Ammonia derivatives (Schiff's base, Oximes, Hydrazones).

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Famous Named Reactions (Mandatory)
1. **Aldol Condensation**: Aldehydes/Ketones with **$\alpha$-Hydrogen**. Forms $\beta$-hydroxy carbonyls.
2. **Cannizzaro Reaction**: Aldehydes with **No $\alpha$-Hydrogen** (e.g., HCHO, Benzaldehyde). Undergoes self-oxidation/reduction to alcohol and acid salt.
3. **Clemmensen / Wolff-Kishner**: Reduction of $C=O$ to $CH_2$.

### ⚡ Carboxylic Acids
- **Preparation**: From Nitriles, Grignard reagents ($+ CO_2$), or oxidation of $1^\circ$ alcohols/alkyl benzenes.
- **Acidity**: Presence of Electron Withdrawing Groups (EWG like $-Cl, -NO_2$) increases acidity; Electron Donating Groups (EDG) decrease it.
- **Hell-Volhard-Zelinsky (HVZ)**: $\alpha$-halogenation of carboxylic acids.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Fehling’s / Tollen’s Test**:
  - Aldehydes: **Positive** (Silver mirror / Red ppt).
  - Ketones: **Negative**.
  - Benzaldehyde: Positive for Tollen's, **Negative** for Fehling's.
- **Acidity Order**: $HCOOH > CH_3COOH$ (due to $+I$ effect of methyl group).
- **Aldol vs Cannizzaro**: Check for $\alpha$-Hydrogen first!
