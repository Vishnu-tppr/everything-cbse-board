# 🧬 Ch06 - Haloalkanes and Haloarenes

> **Chemistry** | **Marks**: ~6 | [[🧪 Chemistry]]
> This chapter begins the Organic Chemistry block, focusing on the substitution and elimination reactions of alkyl and aryl halides.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Classification & Nomenclature
- Based on number of halogens (mono, di, poly).
- Nature of $C-X$ bond: $sp^3 C-X$ vs $sp^2 C-X$ (vinyl, aryl).

### 2. Nucleophilic Substitution ($S_N$)
- **$S_N2$ (Bimolecular)**: 
  - Single step; Inversion of configuration (Walden Inversion).
  - Order of reactivity: Primary > Secondary > Tertiary.
- **$S_N1$ (Unimolecular)**: 
  - Two steps (Carbocation formation); Racemization.
  - Order of reactivity: Tertiary > Secondary > Primary.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Chemical Reactions
- **Elimination (Dehydrohalogenation)**: Saytzeff’s Rule (more substituted alkene is major).
- **Reaction with Metals**:
  - **Grignard Reagent**: $R-X + Mg \xrightarrow{dry \, ether} R-MgX$.
  - **Wurtz Reaction**: $2RX + 2Na \to R-R + 2NaX$.
  - **Fittig / Wurtz-Fittig**: For aryl halides.

### ⚡ Nucleophilic Substitution in Haloarenes
- Haloarenes are **extremely less reactive** towards $S_N$ reactions due to:
  1. Resonance effect (partial double bond).
  2. Difference in hybridization of Carbon ($sp^2$).
  3. Instability of phenyl cation.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **$S_N1$ vs $S_N2$ Reactivity**: Always check for steric hindrance ($S_N2$) vs Carbocation stability ($S_N1$).
- **Aqueous vs Alcoholic KOH**:
  - **Aqueous KOH** $\to$ Substitution (Alcohol).
  - **Alcoholic KOH** $\to$ Elimination (Alkene).
- **Optical Activity**: Chiral carbon (4 different groups) and non-superimposable mirror images.
