# ⚗️ Ch01 - Solutions

> **Chemistry** | **Marks**: ~7 | [[🧪 Chemistry]]
> This chapter covers types of solutions, solubility, and the critical Colligative Properties that depend only on the number of solute particles.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Concentration Terms
- **Molarity** ($M$): Moles of solute / Litre of solution. (Temp dependent).
- **Molality** ($m$): Moles of solute / Kg of solvent. (Temp independent).
- **Mole Fraction** ($x$): Ratio of moles of one component to total moles.

### 2. Solubility
- **Henry’s Law**: $p = K_H x$. (Solubility of gas in liquid is proportional to partial pressure).
- **Raoult’s Law**: For a solution of volatile liquids, $p_i = p_i^\circ x_i$.
  - **Ideal Solutions**: $\Delta H_{mix} = 0, \Delta V_{mix} = 0$. Obey Raoult's Law.
  - **Non-Ideal**: Show +ve or -ve deviations.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Colligative Properties (Mandatory)
1. **Relative Lowering of Vapour Pressure**: $\frac{p_1^\circ - p_1}{p_1^\circ} = x_2 \approx \frac{n_2}{n_1}$.
2. **Elevation of Boiling Point**: $\Delta T_b = K_b m$.
3. **Depression of Freezing Point**: $\Delta T_f = K_f m$.
4. **Osmotic Pressure**: $\pi = CRT = \frac{n}{V} RT$.

### ⚡ Van’t Hoff Factor ($i$)
- Used for electrolytes (dissociation/association).
- $i = \frac{\text{Observed Colligative Property}}{\text{Calculated Colligative Property}}$.
- For dissociation: $i > 1$. For association: $i < 1$.
- Modified formulas: $\Delta T_b = i K_b m$, $\pi = i CRT$.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Molarity vs Molality**: Molality is preferred for colligative properties as it doesn't change with temperature.
- **Azeotropes**: Constant boiling mixtures. Minimum boiling = +ve deviation; Maximum boiling = -ve deviation.
- **Solvent vs Solution**: In $\Delta T_f$ or $\Delta T_b$, always calculate based on the **mass of solvent** ($w_1$), not total mass.
