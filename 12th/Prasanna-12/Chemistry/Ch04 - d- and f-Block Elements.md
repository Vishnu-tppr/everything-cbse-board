# ⛓️ Ch04 - d- and f-Block Elements

> **Chemistry** | **Marks**: ~7 | [[🧪 Chemistry]]
> This chapter covers the electronic configuration, properties, and chemical reactivity of transition (d-block) and inner-transition (f-block) elements.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Transition Elements (d-block)
- Elements with partially filled d-orbitals in ground or common oxidation state.
- **Exceptions**: $Zn, Cd, Hg$ are NOT transition elements as they have full $d^{10}$ config.
- **Key Properties**:
  - **Metallic Nature**: High melting points (except $Zn, Cd, Hg$).
  - **Atomic Size**: Decreases till the middle, then constant, then slight increase.
  - **Ionization Enthalpy**: Increases across the series.

### 2. Characteristic Properties
- **Variable Oxidation States**: Due to small energy gap between $ns$ and $(n-1)d$ orbitals.
- **Catalytic Property**: Ability to adopt multiple oxidation states and provide surface area.
- **Magnetic Properties**: Most are paramagnetic due to unpaired electrons. $\mu = \sqrt{n(n+2)} BM$.
- **Formation of Coloured Ions**: Due to d-d transitions in the presence of ligands.
- **Interstitial Compounds**: Small atoms (H, C, N) trapped in crystal lattice.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 f-block Elements
- **Lanthanoids** ($4f$ series): Ce to Lu.
- **Actinoids** ($5f$ series): Th to Lr.
- **Lanthanoid Contraction**: The steady decrease in atomic/ionic radii with increase in atomic number.
  - **Reason**: Poor shielding effect of 4f electrons.
  - **Consequence**: Zr and Hf have almost identical radii; separation of lanthanoids is difficult.

### ⚡ Potassium Permanganate ($KMnO_4$) & Dichromate ($K_2Cr_2O_7$)
- **$K_2Cr_2O_7$**: Orange (dichromate) $\iff$ Yellow (chromate) depending on pH. (Basic $\to$ Chromate; Acidic $\to$ Dichromate).
- **$KMnO_4$**: Strong oxidizing agent in acidic, neutral, and alkaline media.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **$Zn, Cd, Hg$ logic**: Always explain based on the *completely filled d-orbitals*.
- **Lanthanoid vs Actinoid Contraction**: Actinoid contraction is **greater** than lanthanoid contraction due to even poorer shielding of 5f vs 4f.
- **Oxidation state of Mn**: It shows the highest variety ($+2$ to $+7$).
