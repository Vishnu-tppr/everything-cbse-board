# 🕸️ Ch05 - Coordination Compounds

> **Chemistry** | **Marks**: ~7 | [[🧪 Chemistry]]
> This chapter introduces complex compounds where a central metal atom is bonded to ligands through coordinate bonds.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Werner’s Theory
- **Primary Valency**: Ionizable; corresponds to Oxidation State.
- **Secondary Valency**: Non-ionizable; corresponds to Coordination Number. Satisfied by negative or neutral ligands.

### 2. Nomenclature & Isomerism
- **IUPAC Naming**: Cation first, then ligands (alphabetical), then metal (with OS in Roman numerals).
- **Isomerism**:
  - **Structural**: Linkage, Coordination, Ionization, Solvate.
  - **Stereoisomerism**: Geometrical (cis/trans) and Optical (dextro/laevo).

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Bonding Theories (Critical)
1. **Valence Bond Theory (VBT)**:
   - Predicts Hybridization and Geometry ($sp^3 \to$ Tetrahedral, $dsp^2 \to$ Square Planar, $d^2sp^3/sp^3d^2 \to$ Octahedral).
   - Predicts Magnetic behavior (Inner orbital vs Outer orbital).
2. **Crystal Field Theory (CFT)**:
   - Explains splitting of d-orbitals in octahedral and tetrahedral fields.
   - **Spectrochemical Series**: Arranges ligands by field strength ($I^- < Br^- < \dots < H_2O < \dots < CN^- < CO$).
   - **Strong Field Ligands**: Cause pairing ($\Delta_o > P$).
   - **Weak Field Ligands**: No pairing ($\Delta_o < P$).

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Oxidation State Calculation**: Ensure you know the charges of common ligands (e.g., $C_2O_4^{2-}$ is $-2$, $en$ is $0$).
- **VBT Limitations**: VBT does not explain why some complexes are coloured or why some ligands are strong/weak. CFT fixes this.
- **Inner vs Outer Orbital**: In $d^2sp^3$, the $(n-1)d$ orbitals are used; in $sp^3d^2$, the $nd$ orbitals are used.
