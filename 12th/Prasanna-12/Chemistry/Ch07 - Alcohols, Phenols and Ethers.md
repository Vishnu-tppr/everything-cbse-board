# 🍷 Ch07 - Alcohols, Phenols and Ethers

> **Chemistry** | **Marks**: ~6 | [[🧪 Chemistry]]
> This chapter explores the hydroxyl group attached to alkyl (alcohols) and aryl (phenols) groups, and the oxygen linkage (ethers).

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Preparation of Alcohols
- **Acid catalyzed hydration** of alkenes.
- **Hydroboration-Oxidation**: Anti-Markovnikov addition of $H_2O$.
- **Reduction** of Carbonyls (Aldehydes $\to 1^\circ$; Ketones $\to 2^\circ$).

### 2. Phenols
- Preparation from **Cumene** (Industrial method), Haloarenes (Dow’s process), or Diazonium salts.
- **Acidity**: Phenols are more acidic than alcohols due to the stability of the phenoxide ion via resonance.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Chemical Reactions & Named Reactions
- **Dehydration**: $1^\circ$ at $443K$ gives alkene; at $413K$ gives ether.
- **Lucas Test**: $ZnCl_2/HCl$. Distinguishes $1^\circ, 2^\circ, 3^\circ$ alcohols (Turbidity: $3^\circ$ instant, $2^\circ$ in 5 mins, $1^\circ$ only on heating).
- **Phenol Named Reactions**:
  - **Reimer-Tiemann**: Phenol $\to$ Salicylaldehyde ($CHCl_3/NaOH$).
  - **Kolbe’s Reaction**: Phenol $\to$ Salicylic Acid ($CO_2/NaOH$).

### ⚡ Ethers
- **Williamson Synthesis**: $R-X + R'-ONa \to R-O-R'$. (Mechanism is $S_N2$; $R-X$ must be primary).
- **Cleavage with HI**: $R-O-R' + HI \to RI + R'OH$. (If one group is tertiary, alkyl iodide forms from the tertiary group via $S_N1$).

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Boiling Point**: Alcohols have higher BP than ethers/hydrocarbons of comparable mass due to **Intermolecular Hydrogen Bonding**.
- **Solubility**: Decreases with increase in size of alkyl group (hydrophobic part).
- **Williamson Constraint**: Tertiary halides give **alkenes** instead of ethers due to elimination.
