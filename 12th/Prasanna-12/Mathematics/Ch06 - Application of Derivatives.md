# 📐 Ch06 - Application of Derivatives

> **Mathematics** | **Marks**: ~8-10 | [[📘 Mathematics]]
> This chapter applies differentiation to find rates of change, identify increasing/decreasing intervals, and solve optimization problems (Maxima/Minima).

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Rate of Change
- $\frac{dy}{dx}$ represents the rate of change of $y$ with respect to $x$.
- If $x=f(t)$ and $y=g(t)$, then $\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy/dt}{dx/dt}$.

### 2. Increasing and Decreasing Functions
- **Increasing**: $f'(x) > 0$ in interval $I$.
- **Decreasing**: $f'(x) < 0$ in interval $I$.
- **Critical Points**: Where $f'(x) = 0$ or is undefined.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Maxima and Minima (Critical)
1. **First Derivative Test**: Check sign of $f'(x)$ as $x$ passes through critical point $c$.
   - $+ \to -$ : Local Maxima.
   - $- \to +$ : Local Minima.
2. **Second Derivative Test** (Most common in boards):
   - Find $c$ such that $f'(c) = 0$.
   - If $f''(c) < 0$, then $c$ is a point of **Local Maxima**.
   - If $f''(c) > 0$, then $c$ is a point of **Local Minima**.
   - If $f''(c) = 0$, test fails; use 1st derivative test.

### ⚡ Optimization Problems
- Strategy:
  1. Draw diagram and label variables.
  2. Write function to be optimized in terms of one variable (using given constraints).
  3. Differentiate, find critical points, and verify with 2nd derivative.

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Strictly Increasing**: $f'(x) > 0$. If the question says "Increasing", $f'(x) \geq 0$ is usually accepted, but check NCERT context.
- **2nd Derivative Confusion**: $f''(c) < 0$ is **MAXIMA**. Students often associate negative with "minima".
- **Absolute Max/Min**: For a closed interval $[a, b]$, you must check critical points AND the endpoints $a$ and $b$.
