# 📐 Ch08 - Application of Integrals

> **Mathematics** | **Marks**: ~4-6 | [[📘 Mathematics]]
> This chapter uses Definite Integrals to calculate the area bounded by simple curves like lines, parabolas, and circles.

---

## 🎯 Conceptual Core (NCERT Mirror)

### 1. Area under a Simple Curve
- Area bounded by the curve $y = f(x)$, $x$-axis and the lines $x=a$ and $x=b$ is: $Area = \int_a^b |y| dx$.
- Area bounded by $x = g(y)$, $y$-axis and $y=c, y=d$ is: $Area = \int_c^d |x| dy$.

### 2. Area between Two Curves
- Area between $y = f(x)$ and $y = g(x)$ from $x=a$ to $x=b$ (where $f(x) \geq g(x)$) is: $Area = \int_a^b [f(x) - g(x)] dx$.

---

## 📐 The 12th Grade Technicals

### 🧪 Standard Curves (Sketching)
- **Parabola**: $y^2 = 4ax$ (right), $y^2 = -4ax$ (left), $x^2 = 4ay$ (up).
- **Circle**: $x^2 + y^2 = r^2$.
- **Ellipse**: $\frac{x^2}{a^2} + \frac{y^2}{b^2} = 1$.
- **Straight Line**: $y = mx + c$ or intercept form.

### ⚡ Solving Strategy
1. **Sketch the curves**: Identify the region of interest.
2. **Find intersection points**: Solve equations of curves simultaneously.
3. **Decide Strip Orientation**: 
   - Vertical strip ($dx$): Use if region bounded by $x$-axis or top-minus-bottom functions.
   - Horizontal strip ($dy$): Use if region bounded by $y$-axis or right-minus-left functions.
4. **Integrate**: Apply limits and standard formulas (especially $\int \sqrt{a^2-x^2} dx$).

---

## ⚠️ The "Mistake DNA" Alert (Common Traps)
- **Area is always positive**: If the region is below the $x$-axis, the integral will be negative; take the absolute value.
- **Symmetry**: Use symmetry to simplify (e.g., $Area = 4 \times \text{Area of 1st quadrant}$ for circles/ellipses).
- **Standard Formula Error**: The area of an ellipse is $\pi a b$. Use this to verify your final answer!
