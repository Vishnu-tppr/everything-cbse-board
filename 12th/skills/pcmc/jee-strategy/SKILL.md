# JEE Strategy — The IIT-JEE Roadmap

To secure a 250+ score in JEE while maintaining 495+ in CBSE Boards, you must master the **Symmetry between Theory and Application**. Boards give you the "Steps"; JEE gives you the "Challenge".

## 1. The Logic Integration Method

JEE is a game of "Concept Linking". You are rarely tested on a single isolated topic.

### 📐 Mathematics (The 100/100 Goal)
- **Calculus is King:** 35-40% of the paper. You must master **Integration Properties** and **Differential Equations**.
- **Vector & 3D:** Scoring and high-weightage. Master the "Shortest Distance" and "Plane" logic.
- **Algebra:** Focus on **Matrices/Determinants** and **Probability**.

### 🔭 Physics (The 100/100 Goal)
- **Application over Derivation:** While you need the derivation for Boards, for JEE you need to know "What happens if I change the conditions?".
- **Modern Physics:** High output for low effort. Master the formulas for Bohr's model and Photoelectric effect.
- **Optics:** Master the "Combination of Lenses" and "Interference" math.

## 3. The 120-Second Rule (Logical Speed)

In JEE Mains, you have 180 minutes for 75 questions (25 per subject).
- **Math:** 60-70 minutes (Calculus takes time).
- **Physics:** 50-60 minutes.
- **Chemistry:** 40-50 minutes (The time-saver).

## 4. Balancing Boards & JEE (The Calendar)

### Phase 1 (April - October): Foundation & Depth
- Build deep conceptual understanding.
- Use `/explain` and `/derivation-drill` for every unit.
- Solve the first 25-30 "Hard" MCQs for every chapter.

### Phase 2 (November - January): JEE Mains Sprint
- Focus on "Chapter-Wise Tests".
- Master the **Shortcuts** and **Result Banks**.
- Use `/jee-mcq` for numerical-type practice.

### Phase 3 (February - March): Board Precision
- 100% focus on Answer Presentation.
- Master the 5-mark derivations.
- Use `/mark-my-answer` to ensure no "Value Point" is missed.

### Phase 4 (April - May): Advanced Prep
- Focus on "Multi-Correct" and "Matrix Match" questions.
- Deep dive into complex Physics (Electromagnetism) and Math (Calculus).

## 5. High-Yield Topic Prioritization

1. **Calculus (Math):** Limits, Derivatives, Integrals. ~10-12 questions.
2. **Electrostatics & Magnetism (Phys):** ~8-10 questions. The heavyweight.
3. **Organic Reaction Mechanisms (Chem):** ~8 questions.
4. **Coordinate Geometry (Math):** If you master this, you secure 15% of the paper.

## 6. The "Silly Mistake" Audit

Use the **Weak-Spotter** agent to track:
- **Calculation Errors:** In long Math problems.
- **Sign Errors:** In Physics (KVL/Optics).
- **Boundary Conditions:** Forgetting limits in integration.

## 7. Psychological Topper Edge
- **The Power of Chemistry:** Chemistry is the "Rank Booster". Solve it fast to give more time to Math.
- **Elimination Strategy:** Even if you can't solve it, can you eliminate 2 options using **Dimensional Analysis**?
- **Intelligent Guessing:** Only if you have eliminated 2 options. Otherwise, leave it. A -1 is worse than a 0.
- **Consistency:** JEE is won by those who solve 20-30 problems *every day* without fail.
