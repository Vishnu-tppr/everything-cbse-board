# Error Analysis — The Mechanical Killers (12th Grade)

To reach 498+/500, you must eliminate "The Silly Mistakes". These aren't about not knowing the concept; they are about **Mechanical Slips** in execution.

---

## 1. The Sign Convention Slip (Physics/Math)
- **Physics (KVL/Optics):** 
    - ❌ Moving against the current ($+IR$ instead of $-IR$).
    - ❌ Forgetting $u$ is always negative.
- **Math (Integration):** 
    - ❌ Evaluating $\int_a^b$ as $(a - b)$ instead of $(b - a)$.
    - ❌ Swapping signs when transposing Determinant cofactor values.
- **Fix:** Use a **Dedicated Margin** for sign verification before committing to the main answer line.

---

## 2. The Power of 10 & Units (Science)
- **Electrostatics:** 
    - ❌ Forgetting to convert $cm$ to $m$ or $\mu C$ to $C$ in $k q_1 q_2 / r^2$.
- **Kinetics:** 
    - ❌ Mixing $seconds$ and $minutes$ in half-life calculations.
- **Optics:** 
    - ❌ $P = 1/f$ only works if $f$ is in **Meters**.
- **Fix:** Write the **GIVEN** data list with conversions FIRST. 
    - *Example:* $r = 10 \, \text{cm} = 0.1 \, \text{m}$.

---

## 3. The "Missing Constant" (Mathematics)
- **Integrals:** 
    - ❌ Forgetting $+C$ in Indefinite Integrals. (Deducts 0.5M).
- **Differential Eq:** 
    - ❌ Forgetting to multiply the **Integrating Factor (I.F.)** on both sides.
- **Fix:** Every time you see $\int$, your brain should scream "$+C$".

---

## 4. The "Valency/Arrows" Slip (Chemistry)
- **Organic:** 
    - ❌ Drawing a 5-bonded Carbon atom in a mechanism. (Instant 0 marks).
    - ❌ Arrows showing electron flow from positive to negative. (Should be negative $\to$ positive).
- **Inorganic:** 
    - ❌ Forgetting to balance the redox half-reactions in a full equation.
- **Fix:** Count the bonds on every Carbon atom after drawing a structure.

---

## 5. The "Copying" Error
- **Matrices:** 
    - ❌ Copying an element from the question paper incorrectly ($3$ becomes $8$ or $-2$ becomes $2$).
- **Calculus:** 
    - ❌ Copying the exponent wrong in the second step of a long derivation.
- **Fix:** **Finger-Point Check.** Point at the question paper with your left hand while writing with your right.
