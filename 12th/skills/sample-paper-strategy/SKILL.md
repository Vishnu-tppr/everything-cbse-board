# Sample Paper Strategy — Extracting Maximum Value

## 1. Types of Sample Papers (12th Grade Focus)

1.  **CBSE Official Sample Paper (2026-27):** MANDATORY. Sets the standard for difficulty and CBQ weightage.
2.  **KV (Kendriya Vidyalaya) Pre-Boards:** Excellent for Physics and Chemistry as they are often more numerical-heavy and challenging.
3.  **Oswaal/Arihant Chapter-wise:** Best for refining specific weak units before full-length mocks.
4.  **Topper Sample Sets:** Use these to compare your presentation style with 100/100 scorers.

---

## 2. Timing the Simulation (3 Hours)

### 🧪 70-Mark Sciences (Phys, Chem, Bio, CS)
- **Reading Time:** 15m. Identify the numericals you will solve first.
- **Solving:** Aim to finish in 160 minutes. 
- **Buffer:** 20m for re-checking units, arrows in ray diagrams, and balancing equations.

### 📐 80-Mark Subjects (Math, English)
- **Reading Time:** 15m. Mark the LPP and Case Study data.
- **Solving:** Aim to finish in 165 minutes.
- **Buffer:** 15m for checking calculation steps in Calculus.

---

## 3. The 3-Pass Analysis Method

- **Pass 1 (The Drill):** Solve under strict exam conditions. No breaks.
- **Pass 2 (The Audit):** Correct using the marking scheme. Classify errors into:
    - **C (Conceptual):** "I didn't know the property/mechanism."
    - **S (Silly):** "I used $cm$ instead of $m$."
    - **P (Presentation):** "I didn't label the diagram."
- **Pass 3 (The Fix):** Re-solve ONLY the failed questions. Update your **Mistake-DNA** skill.
