# Marking Scheme Decoded — 12th Grade Edition

The difference between a 95 and a 100 is knowing **Where the marks are hidden**. CBSE examiners follow a strict "Value Point" key.

---

## 1. Step-Marking Breakdown

### 🔭 Physics (70 Marks)
- **Derivations (3-5M):**
    - **Diagram:** 1M (Mandatory. No diagram = -1M even if derivation is correct).
    - **Initial Assumption/Theory:** 0.5M.
    - **Logical Steps:** 1M - 2.5M.
    - **Final Expression (Boxed):** 0.5M.
- **Numericals:**
    - **Formula:** 1M.
    - **Substitution:** 0.5M.
    - **Final Result with Unit:** 0.5M. (❌ Wrong unit = -0.5M).

### 🧪 Chemistry (70 Marks)
- **Organic Mechanisms (3M):**
    - **Electron Flow Arrows:** 1M.
    - **Intermediate Structure:** 1M.
    - **Final Product:** 1M.
- **Distinction Tests:**
    - **Reagent Name:** 0.5M.
    - **Observations for both compounds:** 1M + 1M.
- **Numerical:** (Similar to Physics - Formula + Substitution + Answer).

### 📐 Mathematics (80 Marks)
- **Calculus Proofs (5M):**
    - **Correct Property Mention:** 1M.
    - **Intermediate Steps:** 3M.
    - **Final Result/Therefore statement:** 1M.
- **Case Study (4M):** 1M + 1M + 2M (Stepwise).

---

## 2. Subject-Specific "Value Point" Targets

### 🧬 Biology (70 Marks)
- **Keywords:** Use terms like *Parthenocarpy*, *Semiconservative*, *Bioreactor*. (❌ Simple synonyms get 0 marks).
- **Diagrams:** Must be labeled. An unlabeled diagram in a 5-mark question reduces the score significantly.

### 💻 Computer Science (70 Marks)
- **Indentation:** Python code must be visually indented even on paper.
- **SQL:** Query accuracy (1M per query). Correct use of `GROUP BY` and `HAVING`.

### 📖 English Core (80 Marks)
- **Format:** Notice/Letter/Report (1M for Format).
- **Content:** 2M for thematic depth in Literature.
- **Expression:** 2M for grammar and vocabulary.

---

## 3. The "Half-Mark" Killers
Examiners are instructed to deduct **0.5 Marks** for:
- Missing Units (e.g., writing $5$ instead of $5 \, \text{Tesla}$).
- Missing Directions in Ray Diagrams (arrows).
- Missing Constant of Integration ($+C$) in Math.
- Overwriting/Messy strike-throughs.

---

## 💡 The "Senior Sibling" Advice
If you don't know the full answer, write the **Formula** and the **Given Data**. You will secure 1 to 1.5 marks. Never leave a sheet blank. The examiner *wants* to give you marks, but they need a "Value Point" to anchor them to.
