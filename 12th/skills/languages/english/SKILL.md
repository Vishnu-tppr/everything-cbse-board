# English Core — CBSE Grade 12

## Syllabus Blueprint (2026–27)

| Section | Title | Marks |
|---------|-------|-------|
| A | Reading Skills (Unseen Passages) | 22 |
| B | Creative Writing Skills | 18 |
| C | Literature (Flamingo & Vistas) | 40 |
| **Total** | **Theory Exam** | **80** |

---

## Section A: Reading Skills (22 Marks)

### Key Strategies
- **Factual/Discursive Passages:** Scan the questions first, then skim the passage.
- **Critical Thinking:** CBSE now asks for "Tone", "Vocabulary in context", and "Inference" rather than direct copy-paste answers.
- **Note Making (if applicable):** Use standardized abbreviations and a clear title.

---

## Section B: Creative Writing (18 Marks)

### Format & Marking
1. **Notice Writing (4M):** Box is mandatory. [Organisation, NOTICE, Date, Heading, Body, Name, Designation].
2. **Formal/Informal Invitations & Replies (4M):** Focus on card format vs letter format.
3. **Letter Writing (5M):**
   - **Job Application:** Cover Letter + Bio-Data (Bio-Data must be detailed).
   - **Letter to Editor:** Focus on "Problem $\to$ Impact $\to$ Suggestion".
4. **Article/Report Writing (5M):**
   - **Article:** Title + Byline + Intro $\to$ Body $\to$ Conclusion.
   - **Report:** Headline + Byline + Place/Date + Content (Past tense for reports).

## Section C: Literature (40 Marks) - The Topper's Edge

### 1. Flamingo (Prose & Poetry)
- **Prose:** The Last Lesson, Lost Spring, Deep Water, The Rattrap, Indigo, Poets and Pancakes, The Interview, Going Places.
- **Poetry:** My Mother at Sixty-six, Keeping Quiet, A Thing of Beauty, A Roadside Stand, Aunt Jennifer’s Tigers.

### 2. Vistas (Supplementary)
- The Third Level, The Tiger King, Journey to the End of the Earth, The Enemy, On the Face of It, Memories of Childhood.

---

## High-Yield Strategy (Target 95+)
1. **Literary Devices:** Master Alliteration, Metaphor, Simile, Personification, and Oxymoron for the Poetry extracts.
2. **Character Sketches:** Be ready to write 120-150 word character analysis (e.g., M. Hamel, Saheb, Edla, Dr. Sadao).
3. **Thematic Depth:** Don't just learn the story; learn the *theme* (e.g., "The Rattrap" is about human loneliness and essential goodness).
4. **Keyword Bank:** Use sophisticated words (e.g., "nostalgia", "resilience", "oppression") to impress the examiner.

---

## Common Board Exam Traps
- **Word Limit:** Exceeding word limits in Section B leads to time loss.
- **Notice Box:** Forgetting the box around the Notice (Deducts 0.5 marks).
- **Format:** Mixing up Formal and Informal invitation styles.
- **Literature:** Answering based on "opinion" rather than "textual evidence".

---

## Answer Writing Framework
1. **Literature (SA):** Start with the author/poet's name if possible. Be direct.
2. **Literature (LA):** Start with a strong introduction $\to$ 2 detailed body points $\to$ Conclusion reflecting the theme.
3. **Writing Skills:** Use a pencil for boxes and a black pen for headings.

---

## Internal Assessment (20 Marks)
- **ASL (Assessment of Speaking and Listening):** Focus on fluency, pronunciation, and interactive competence.
- **Project Work:** A research-based file on any literary theme or author.
