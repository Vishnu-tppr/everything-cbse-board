# Word Budget Rules

## Answer-Length Calibration

Students lose marks two ways:
1. **Too short** — 3-mark answer written in 2 lines (missing value points)
2. **Too long** — 2-mark answer written in 10 lines (wasted time, no extra marks)

### Enforce These Word Budgets

| Marks | Word Budget | Lines | Time Budget | Structure |
|-------|------------|-------|-------------|-----------|
| 1M (MCQ) | Select option | 0 | 1-1.5 min | Tick/circle only |
| 1M (VSA) | 15-20 words | 1-2 | 1-2 min | One clear sentence |
| 2M | 30-40 words | 3-4 | 3-4 min | Definition + Application |
| 3M | 50-75 words | 5-8 | 5-7 min | 3 distinct points |
| 5M | 100-120 words | 10-15 | 10-12 min | 5 points + diagram |
| 4M (Case) | 60-80 words | 6-10 | 8-10 min | 3 sub-parts answered |

### Subject-Specific Calibration

```
MATHEMATICS:
  Don't count words — count STEPS
  2M = 3-4 steps | 3M = 5-6 steps | 5M = 7-10 steps + diagram
  Show every intermediate step (that's where marks are)

SCIENCE:
  2M = 1 definition + 1 example OR diagram
  3M = 3 points OR 2 points + diagram
  5M = concept + 3-4 points + labeled diagram + conclusion

SOCIAL SCIENCE:
  2M = 2 facts with dates/names (1 point = 1 mark)
  3M = 3 facts with context
  5M = 5 points under sub-headings (Causes/Methods/Impact)

ENGLISH:
  Literature SA = 40-60 words with textual quote
  Literature LA = 100-120 words with 2+ quotes
  Letter = EXACTLY 100-120 words (format itself = marks)
  Paragraph = EXACTLY 100-120 words (data from source)
```

### Time Check Rule

If you've spent more than the time budget on a question:
1. Finish the current point
2. Move on
3. Come back in review time if available
4. A partial 5M answer (3/5) + a complete next answer (3/3) = 6 marks
   vs. an over-written 5M answer (4/5) + skipped next answer (0/3) = 4 marks

### Over-Writing Penalty (Time, Not Marks)

CBSE doesn't deduct for writing too much, but:
- You waste 2-3 minutes per over-written answer
- Over 30 questions × 2 min wasted = 60 minutes GONE
- That's an entire section you might not finish
- **The #1 cause of "I couldn't finish the paper" = over-writing early questions**
