# Ch14 — Probability

> **Unit VII** | **Marks**: 3-5 (every paper) | [[📘 Mathematics]]

## Key Concepts

- P(E) = Favorable outcomes / Total outcomes
- P(E) + P(not E) = 1
- 0 ≤ P(E) ≤ 1
- Impossible event: P = 0, Sure event: P = 1

## Key Formulas

```
P(E) = Favorable outcomes / Total outcomes
P(not E) = 1 - P(E)

Playing cards: 52 total (26 red, 26 black, 4 suits of 13)
  Face cards: 12 (4 Jacks, 4 Queens, 4 Kings)
  Aces: 4
Dice: 6 outcomes (1-6)
Coins: 2 outcomes (H, T)
Two dice: 36 outcomes
Two coins: 4 outcomes (HH, HT, TH, TT)
```

## Common Exam Traps ⚠️

1. Not counting total outcomes correctly for cards/dice
2. Forgetting that P(not E) = 1 - P(E) is the fastest method
3. Confusing "at least one" with "exactly one"
