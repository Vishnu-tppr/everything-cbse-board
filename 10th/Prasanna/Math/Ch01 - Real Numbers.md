# Ch01 — Real Numbers

> **Unit I: Number Systems** | **Marks**: 6 | [[📘 Mathematics]]

## Key Concepts

- **Euclid's Division Lemma**: a = bq + r, where 0 ≤ r < b
- **Euclid's Division Algorithm** for finding HCF
- **Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic**: every composite number = product of primes (unique factorization)
- Finding LCM and HCF using prime factorization
- **Relationship**: HCF(a,b) × LCM(a,b) = a × b
- Proving irrationality of √2, √3, √5 (contradiction method)
- Decimal expansions: terminating ↔ denominator has only 2s and 5s as factors

## Key Formulas

```
HCF × LCM = Product of two numbers (ONLY for two numbers)
Euclid's Division Lemma: a = bq + r (0 ≤ r < b)
Terminating decimal: denominator = 2ⁿ × 5ᵐ
```

## Common Exam Traps ⚠️

1. HCF × LCM = Product works ONLY for **two** numbers
2. Not showing the contradiction step clearly in irrationality proofs
3. Confusing terminating vs non-terminating conditions
