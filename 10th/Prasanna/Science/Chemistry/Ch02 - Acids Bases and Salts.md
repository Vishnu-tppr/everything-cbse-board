# Ch02 — Acids, Bases and Salts

> **Chemistry** | **Marks**: Part of 25 | [[🔬 Science]]

## Key Concepts

- Acid → H⁺ ions in water; Base → OH⁻ ions
- pH scale: 0-14 (< 7 acidic, = 7 neutral, > 7 basic)
- Indicators: litmus, methyl orange, phenolphthalein

## pH Scale Reference

```
pH 0-1:   Strong acid (HCl, H₂SO₄)
pH 2-3:   Weak acid (vinegar, lemon)
pH 7:     Neutral (pure water)
pH 8-9:   Slightly basic (baking soda, blood pH 7.35-7.45)
pH 12-14: Strong base (NaOH, KOH)

Tooth enamel decays below pH 5.5
Stomach pH: 1.5-3.5 (HCl)
```

## Important Salts

```
BAKING SODA: NaHCO₃ — antacid, fire extinguisher, baking
WASHING SODA: Na₂CO₃·10H₂O
BLEACHING POWDER: CaOCl₂ — Ca(OH)₂ + Cl₂ → CaOCl₂ + H₂O
PLASTER OF PARIS: CaSO₄·½H₂O — sets by absorbing water
CHLOR-ALKALI: 2NaCl + 2H₂O →electrolysis→ 2NaOH + Cl₂ + H₂
```

## Common Exam Traps ⚠️

1. Confusing NaHCO₃ (baking soda) with Na₂CO₃ (washing soda)
2. pH of blood, stomach — frequently asked MCQs
3. Forgetting chlor-alkali process products
