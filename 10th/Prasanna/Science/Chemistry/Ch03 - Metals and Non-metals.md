# Ch03 — Metals and Non-metals

> **Chemistry** | **Marks**: Part of 25 | [[🔬 Science]]

## Reactivity Series (MUST MEMORIZE)

```
K > Na > Ca > Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Ni > Sn > Pb > [H] > Cu > Hg > Ag > Au > Pt

Mnemonic: "King Nathu Can Make A Zoo In Nagpur Since Pankaj
           Has Cupboard Having Silver And Gold Plus Platinum"

RULES:
- More reactive metal displaces less reactive from its salt
- Metals ABOVE H react with dilute acids → H₂
- Metals BELOW H do NOT react with dilute acids
```

## Extraction Methods

```
HIGH REACTIVITY (K, Na, Ca, Mg, Al): Electrolytic reduction
MEDIUM REACTIVITY (Zn, Fe, Ni, Sn, Pb): Reduction with carbon
LOW REACTIVITY (Cu, Hg, Ag, Au): Self-reduction / heating

Steps: Mining → Enrichment → Roasting/Calcination → Reduction → Refining
  Roasting: sulphide ore → oxide (in air)
  Calcination: carbonate ore → oxide (heat)
```

## Ionic Bonding

```
Na → Na⁺ + e⁻     Cl + e⁻ → Cl⁻     → NaCl
Properties: High MP/BP, soluble in water, conduct electricity
            in molten/aqueous (NOT solid), hard but brittle
```

## Common Exam Traps ⚠️

1. Not remembering exact reactivity series order
2. Confusing roasting (sulphide) with calcination (carbonate)
3. Ionic compounds DON'T conduct electricity in solid state
