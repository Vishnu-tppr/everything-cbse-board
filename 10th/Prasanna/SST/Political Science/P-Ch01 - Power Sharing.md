# P-Ch01 — Power Sharing

> **Political Science** | **Marks**: Part of 20 | [[🌍 Social Science]]

## Key Concepts

- **Belgium model**: accommodation through power sharing
- **Sri Lanka model**: majoritarianism → civil war
- Power sharing is desirable: reduces conflict, ensures stability, moral duty

## Belgium vs Sri Lanka

| Aspect | Belgium | Sri Lanka |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Communities | Dutch (59%), French (40%), German (1%) | Sinhala (74%), Tamil (18%) |
| Approach | Power sharing | Majoritarianism |
| Result | Peace, unity | Civil war (1983-2009) |
| Government | Community government, equal cabinet | Sinhala-only policies |

## Forms of Power Sharing

```
1. HORIZONTAL: Legislature, Executive, Judiciary (checks & balances)
2. VERTICAL: Central, State, Local governments (federalism)
3. AMONG SOCIAL GROUPS: Community government (Belgium), reserved seats
4. AMONG PARTIES: Coalition government, political competition
```

## Common Exam Traps ⚠️

1. Confusing Belgium and Sri Lanka details
2. Not listing all 4 forms of power sharing
3. Forgetting community government concept
