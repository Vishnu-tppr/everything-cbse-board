# P-Ch04 — Political Parties

> **Political Science** | **Marks**: 3-5 | [[🌍 Social Science]]

## National Parties (recognized in 4+ states)

| Party | Symbol | Founded | Key Ideology |
|-------|--------|---------|-------------|
| BJP | Lotus | 1980 | Cultural nationalism, Hindutva |
| INC | Hand | 1885 | Secularism, social justice |
| BSP | Elephant | 1984 | Social justice for Dalits/oppressed |
| CPI(M) | Hammer & Sickle | 1964 | Marxism-Leninism |
| NCP | Clock | 1999 | Democracy, Gandhian secularism |
| AAP | Broom | 2012 | Anti-corruption, transparency |

## Functions of Political Parties

1. Contest elections
2. Form and run government
3. Act as opposition
4. Shape public opinion
5. Access to government machinery

## Challenges Facing Parties

```
1. Dynastic succession (family-based leadership)
2. Internal democracy lacking
3. Money and muscle power in elections
4. No meaningful policy differences
5. Growing role of money in politics
```

## Reforms Suggested

- Anti-defection law, state funding of elections
- RTI for parties, women's reservation
- Independent candidates

## Common Exam Traps ⚠️

1. Not knowing party symbols and founders
2. Confusing national vs state parties criteria
3. One-sided answers on challenges (must also mention reforms)
