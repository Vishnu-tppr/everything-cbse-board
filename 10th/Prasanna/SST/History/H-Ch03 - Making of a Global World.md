# H-Ch03 — The Making of a Global World

> **History** | **Marks**: 3-4 | [[🌍 Social Science]]

## Key Concepts

- **Silk Routes**: connected Asia, Europe, Africa (trade + culture)
- **Colonialism**: European expansion, plantations, forced labor
- **19th century Global Economy**: Corn Laws repeal, food flows
- **Great Depression (1929-35)**: US stock crash → global downturn
- **Bretton Woods (1944)**: IMF + World Bank established

## Key Terms

```
INDENTURED LABOUR: Bonded workers from India → Caribbean, Fiji
CORN LAWS: Protected British agriculture → repeal opened trade
GREAT DEPRESSION: Overproduction → falling prices → unemployment
BRETTON WOODS: Post-war financial system (IMF, World Bank)
G-77: Developing countries demanded NIEO (New International Economic Order)
```

## Common Exam Traps ⚠️

1. Not explaining the impact of Great Depression on India
2. Confusing IMF and World Bank roles
3. Forgetting the role of indentured labour
