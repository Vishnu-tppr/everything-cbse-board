# H-Ch05 — Print Culture and the Modern World

> **History** | **Marks**: 3-4 | [[🌍 Social Science]]

## Key Concepts

- **Gutenberg (1440s)**: movable type printing press → revolutionized Europe
- Print and Reformation: Martin Luther's 95 Theses (1517)
- Print in India: first press by Portuguese missionaries in Goa (1550s)
- **Vernacular Press Act (1878)**: censored Indian-language newspapers
- Raja Ram Mohan Roy: reformer, used print for social change

## Timeline

| Year | Event |

## Key Terms

```
CALLIGRAPHY: beautiful handwriting (pre-print China/Japan)
COMPOSITOR: person who arranges type for printing
CHAPBOOK: cheap, small pocket-sized books sold by chapmen
VELLUM: parchment from animal skin (medieval books)
SEDITIOUS: writings that incite rebellion
```

## Common Exam Traps ⚠️

1. Not mentioning Gutenberg by name
2. Forgetting Vernacular Press Act year (1878)
3. Confusing pre-print methods (woodblock vs movable type)
