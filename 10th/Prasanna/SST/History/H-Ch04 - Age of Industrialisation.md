# H-Ch04 — The Age of Industrialisation

> **History** | **Marks**: 3-4 | [[🌍 Social Science]]

## Key Concepts

- Proto-industrialisation: rural production before factories
- **Spinning Jenny** (1764) — James Hargreaves
- Life of workers: low wages, harsh conditions, seasonal employment
- Indian textiles: decline due to British imports, railway impact
- Bombay and Calcutta as industrial cities

## Key Terms

```
PROTO-INDUSTRIALISATION: Production in countryside before factory system
SPINNING JENNY: sped up spinning → reduced need for spinners
FLYSHUTTLE: sped up weaving → increased demand for yarn
STAPLER: person who "stapled" (sorted) wool by fibre
JOBBER: middleman who recruited workers for factories
```

## India's Industrialisation

```
Pre-colonial: India = major textile exporter
Colonial rule: Indian raw materials → British factories → finished goods back to India
Early factories: Bombay (cotton), Bengal (jute)
Key figures: Dwarkanath Tagore, Jamsetji Tata, Seth Hukumchand
```

## Common Exam Traps ⚠️

1. Not explaining why Indian textiles declined
2. Confusing proto-industrialisation with industrialisation
3. Forgetting the role of the "jobber"
