# E-Ch03 — Money and Credit

> **Economics** | **Marks**: 3-4 | [[🌍 Social Science]]

## Formal vs Informal Credit

| Aspect | Formal | Informal |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Source | Banks, cooperatives | Moneylenders, traders, relatives |
| Interest | Low (regulated by RBI) | Very high (unregulated) |
| Documentation | Required | Often none |
| Supervision | RBI monitors | No supervision |
| Risk | Low | High (debt trap) |

## Key Concepts

```
COLLATERAL: Asset pledged against a loan (land, property, gold)
TERMS OF CREDIT: Interest rate + collateral + documentation + mode of repayment
DEBT TRAP: When borrowing leads to more borrowing at high interest
SHG (Self Help Group):
  - 15-20 members (usually women)
  - Pool savings, lend to each other
  - After 1-2 years → eligible for bank loan
  - Example: Grameen Bank (Bangladesh, Muhammad Yunus)
```

## RBI's Role

- Supervises formal sector banks
- Sets interest rates
- Ensures banks maintain cash reserves
- Cannot regulate informal sector

## Common Exam Traps ⚠️

1. Not defining collateral properly
2. Forgetting SHG details (15-20 members, pooled savings)
3. Not explaining WHY informal credit is dangerous
