# E-Ch02 — Sectors of the Indian Economy ⭐

> **Economics** | **Marks**: 4-5 | [[🌍 Social Science]]
> ⭐ **HIGH YIELD** — data tables, NREGA

## Three Sectors

| Sector | Activity | Examples | GDP Share |
|--------|----------|---------|-----------|
| Primary | Nature-based | Agriculture, fishing, mining | ~14% |
| Secondary | Manufacturing | Factories, construction | ~26% |
| Tertiary | Services | IT, banking, transport, teaching | ~60% |

## Organized vs Unorganized

| Aspect | Organized | Unorganized |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| Registration | Registered under law | Not registered |
| Rules | Follows labor laws | No rules |
| Benefits | PF, medical, holidays | None |
| Job security | High | Low |
| Examples | Government, big companies | Street vendors, daily wage |

## Key Schemes

```
MGNREGA (2005): 100 days guaranteed employment per year
  - Rural households, unskilled manual work
  - If no work within 15 days → unemployment allowance
  - 1/3 beneficiaries must be women

MIDDAY MEAL: Free lunch in government schools
  - Increases enrollment + attendance
  - Reduces malnutrition
```

## Common Exam Traps ⚠️

1. Not mentioning GDP share data
2. Confusing organized and unorganized sectors
3. Forgetting MGNREGA details (100 days, rural, 2005)
