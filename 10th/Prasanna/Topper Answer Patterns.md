# Topper Answer Patterns

> **Exam Strategy** | Maximize marks with perfect presentation | [[🎯 Strategy Hub]]

## 1. The "Rule of 3" for 3-Mark Questions
Never write a single paragraph for a 3-mark question.
- **Point 1**: Direct answer to the question (Core concept)
- **Point 2**: Explanation or supporting detail
- **Point 3**: Example, consequence, or quote
*(Always use bullet points)*

## 2. The "5-Point Structure" for 5-Mark LA
- **Introduction (1 mark)**: Define the main term or state the context.
- **Body Paragraphs / Bullets (3 marks)**: Minimum 3 distinct points with headings. Underline the keywords.
- **Conclusion (1 mark)**: Summary sentence starting with "Therefore," "Hence," or "In conclusion."

## 3. Math Proof Template (Geometry)
Every geometry proof (like BPT or Pythagoras) MUST have:
1. **Given**: What is stated in the question.
2. **To Prove**: The equation/statement to be proved.
3. **Construction** (if any): Dotted lines in the diagram.
4. **Proof**: Left side (Statements) | Right side (Reasons/Theorems in brackets).
5. **Conclusion**: "Hence Proved."
*(Draw the diagram on the right side using a pencil.)*

## 4. Science Numerical Template (Physics)
1. **Given**: List all values with units (e.g., u = -20 cm, f = 10 cm).
2. **To Find**: What is asked (e.g., v = ?, m = ?).
3. **Formula**: Write the base formula (e.g., 1/f = 1/v + 1/u).
4. **Substitution & Calculation**: Step-by-step.
5. **Final Answer**: Box the final answer with proper units and sign.
6. **Nature**: If asked, state "Real, inverted, diminished."

## 5. SST Keyword Strategy
- Don't write stories. Use specific historical/geographical terms.
- Examples: Instead of "many countries came together," write "The Bretton Woods conference established the IMF."
- Underline the key terms (e.g., _Satyagraha_, _Decentralization_, _Kharif_).

## 6. English Literature Extract Strategy
- Identify the chapter and author immediately.
- Quote 1-2 words from the text to support your answer.
- Don't just summarize the story; answer the specific trait or emotion asked.
