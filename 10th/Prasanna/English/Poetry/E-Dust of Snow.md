# E-Dust of Snow

> **Poet**: Robert Frost | [[📖 English]]

## Text
> The way a crow / Shook down on me / The dust of snow / From a hemlock tree
> Has given my heart / A change of mood / And saved some part / Of a day I had rued

## Theme
A small, seemingly insignificant natural event can change your entire mood.

## Analysis
- **Crow**: usually symbolizes bad omen → Frost uses it positively
- **Hemlock tree**: associated with poison → yet brings positive change
- **Dust of snow**: tiny event → big emotional impact
- **Message**: Nature has the power to heal, even in small ways

## Literary Devices
- Alliteration: "dust of snow", "has given my heart"
- Symbolism: crow = negativity, hemlock = sorrow → subverted
- Rhyme scheme: abab

## Exam Q Patterns
- Extract: What change of mood is the poet referring to?
- SA: How does a simple moment change the poet's mood?
- SA: What is the significance of using "crow" and "hemlock"?
