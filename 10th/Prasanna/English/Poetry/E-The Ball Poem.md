# E-The Ball Poem

> **Poet**: John Berryman | [[📖 English]]

## Theme
Loss, growing up, understanding the "epistemology of loss"

## Key Points
- A young boy loses his ball in the water
- The poet doesn't offer him another ball or money ("a dime, another ball, is worthless")
- Why? Because the boy must learn his first lesson of responsibility in a "world of possessions"
- The ball symbolizes his childhood, innocence, and memories which, once lost, cannot be bought back
- He must learn to stand up and accept loss

## Literary Devices
- Symbolism: Ball = childhood/innocence/possessions
- Repetition: "What, what is he to do?", "Balls, balls will be lost always"
- Blank verse: No rhyme scheme (reflects the serious, conversational tone)

## Exam Q Patterns
- Extract: Why does the poet say "I would not intrude on him"?
- SA: What is the "epistemology of loss"?
- Value: Why are money or a new ball "worthless" to the boy?
