# E-The Tale of Custard the Dragon

> **Poet**: Ogden Nash | [[📖 English]]

## Theme
Appearance vs Reality, true courage

## Key Points
- **Belinda**: lives in a white house, brave "as a barrel full of bears"
- **Ink** (black kitten), **Blink** (grey mouse), **Mustard** (yellow dog): all boast about being brave
- **Custard** (dragon): has big teeth, spikes, breathes fire, but cries for a "nice safe cage" (considered a coward)
- A pirate enters through the window
- Belinda cries for help; Ink, Blink, Mustard run away in fear
- Custard snorts like an engine, clashes his tail, and eats the pirate
- Afterwards, everyone goes back to boasting, and Custard goes back to crying for a cage

## Literary Devices
- Simile: "mouth like a fireplace", "brave as a tiger"
- Irony: The "cowardly" dragon is the only brave one when real danger strikes
- Ballad form: A narrative poem telling a story
