# E-Fire and Ice

> **Poet**: Robert Frost | [[📖 English]]

## Text/Theme
The poem discusses how the world will end — either in "fire" (desire, passion, greed) or in "ice" (hatred, coldness, indifference). Both are destructive enough to end the world.

## Key Points
- **Fire** = uncontrolled desires, greed, conflict
- **Ice** = hatred, intolerance, insensitivity
- Frost says he has tasted desire ("fire"), but knows enough of hate ("ice") to say it's equally destructive
- Human emotions, if left unchecked, will destroy humanity

## Literary Devices
- Metaphor: Fire for desire, Ice for hatred
- Personification: Fire and Ice are given power to destroy
- Rhyme scheme: aba abc bcb

## Exam Q Patterns
- Extract: What do "fire" and "ice" stand for?
- SA: How will the world end twice according to the poet?
- Value: What message does the poet convey about human emotions?
