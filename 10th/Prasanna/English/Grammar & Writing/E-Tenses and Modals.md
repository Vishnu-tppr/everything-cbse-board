# E-Tenses and Modals

> **Grammar** | **Marks**: Part of 10 | [[📖 English]]

## Tenses Quick Rules

| Tense | Structure | Usage Example |
|-------|-----------|---------------|
| Simple Present | Subject + V1 (s/es) | Universal truth, habits (The sun rises. He plays.) |
| Present Continuous | is/am/are + V1+ing | Happening right now (I am studying.) |
| Present Perfect | has/have + V3 | Just finished, past event relevant now (I have eaten.) |
| Simple Past | Subject + V2 | Completed past event (I went to school.) |
| Past Continuous | was/were + V1+ing | Was happening in past (I was reading.) |
| Past Perfect | had + V3 | First of two past events (Train had left before I reached.) |
| Simple Future | will/shall + V1 | Future event (I will go.) |

## Modals Quick Rules

| Modal | Usage / Meaning | Example |
|-------|-----------------|---------|
| Can / Could | Ability, informal request | I can swim. Could you help? |
| May / Might | Possibility, formal request | May I come in? It might rain. |
| Must / Have to | Necessity, strong obligation | You must wear a seatbelt. |
| Should / Ought to| Advice, moral duty | You should study. We ought to respect elders. |
| Will / Would | Certainty, polite request | I will pass. Would you like some tea? |

## Exam Traps ⚠️
1. **Subject-Verb Agreement with Tenses**: "He go" ❌ "He goes" ✅
2. **Modals ALWAYS take V1**: "He must went" ❌ "He must go" ✅
3. **Past Perfect sequence**: "When I reached the station, the train left" ❌ "the train HAD left" ✅
