# E-Subject-Verb Concord

> **Grammar** | **Marks**: Part of 10 | [[📖 English]]

## Golden Rule
- Singular Subject = Singular Verb (has 's') → *He plays.*
- Plural Subject = Plural Verb (no 's') → *They play.*

## Tricky Rules to Memorize

1. **Words joined by "and"**: Take a plural verb.
   *Ram and Shyam ARE friends.* (Exception: If they refer to one idea: *Bread and butter IS my favorite breakfast.*)

2. **Or, Nor, Either...or, Neither...nor**: The verb agrees with the subject CLOSER to it.
   *Neither the teacher nor the students WERE there.*
   *Neither the students nor the teacher WAS there.*

3. **As well as, Along with, Together with**: The verb agrees with the FIRST subject.
   *The principal, along with the teachers, IS present.*

4. **Each, Every, Either, Neither, Everyone, Someone, Nobody**: Always take a SINGULAR verb.
   *Every boy and girl WAS given a prize.*
   *Neither of the two books IS useful.*

5. **Words that look plural but are singular**: Mathematics, Physics, News, Measles, Billiards.
   *The news IS bad.*

6. **Collective Nouns**: Usually singular.
   *The committee HAS decided.*

7. **A number of vs The number of**:
   *A number of* students ARE absent. (Plural)
   *The number of* students IS fifty. (Singular)
