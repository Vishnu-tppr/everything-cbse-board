# E-The Midnight Visitor

> **Footprints Without Feet — Ch 3** | Author: **Robert Arthur** | [[📖 English]]

## Theme
Presence of mind and intelligence are more important than physical appearance

## Summary
Ausable is a secret agent who is fat and sloppy — not like a typical spy. Fowler, a young writer, is disappointed by him. In Ausable's hotel room, another spy, Max, points a gun at them, demanding a secret report. Ausable casually invents a story about a non-existent balcony outside the window. When someone knocks (the waiter), Ausable says it's the police. Max jumps out the window onto the "balcony" and falls to his death.

## Key Points
- **Ausable**: fat, sloppy, speaks with an American accent, but has brilliant presence of mind
- **Fowler**: romantic writer looking for typical spy thrills
- **Max**: slender, has a gun, but gets easily outsmarted
- The "police" was just a waiter bringing drinks Ausable had ordered.

## Exam Q Patterns
- SA: How was Ausable different from other secret agents?
- SA: How did Ausable outsmart Max?
- LA: Presence of mind is a valuable asset. Discuss based on the story.
