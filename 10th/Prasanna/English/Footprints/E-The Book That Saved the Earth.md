# E-The Book That Saved the Earth

> **Footprints Without Feet — Ch 9** | Author: **Claire Boiko** | [[📖 English]]

## Theme
Humor, misinterpretation, the power of a book

## Summary
A comedic play set in the 25th century. Historian tells how Earth was saved from a Martian invasion in 2040. Think-Tank, the arrogant ruler of Mars, plans to invade Earth. His crew lands in a library, which they think is a "storage barn." They misinterpret books as sandwiches, communication devices, and finally eye-communication devices. Noodle reads nursery rhymes ("Mother Goose"). Think-Tank takes the rhymes literally (cow jumping over the moon, Humpty Dumpty looking like him) and gets so terrified of Earth's "advanced technology" that he cancels the invasion and flees to Alpha Centauri.

## Key Points
- **Think-Tank**: Arrogant, vain (loves his big balloon head), stupid but thinks he's a genius.
- **Noodle**: Smart, polite, actually runs things but gives credit to Think-Tank.
- A simple book of nursery rhymes saved Earth.

## Exam Q Patterns
- SA: Why did Think-Tank decide not to invade Earth?
- SA: How does Noodle correct Think-Tank without offending him?
- LA: Describe how the Martian crew interpreted the items in the library.
