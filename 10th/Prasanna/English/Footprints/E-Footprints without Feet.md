# E-Footprints without Feet

> **Footprints Without Feet — Ch 5** | Author: **H.G. Wells** | [[📖 English]]

## Theme
Misuse of science, brilliance without morality

## Summary
Griffin is a brilliant scientist who discovers how to make the human body transparent. However, he is a lawless person. He sets his landlord's house on fire and escapes by becoming invisible (naked). To escape the London winter cold, he steals clothes and food from a store, then money from a clergyman in Iping. When caught, he takes off his clothes and escapes again as an invisible man.

## Key Points
- **Griffin**: brilliant scientist but lawless, quick-tempered, and selfish
- Misuses his discovery for stealing and revenge, not for the benefit of humanity
- Being invisible is a disadvantage in winter (must be completely naked)

## Exam Q Patterns
- SA: How did Griffin become invisible? Why did he become a homeless wanderer?
- SA: What happened in the clergyman's study?
- LA: Griffin was rather a lawless person. Comment.
