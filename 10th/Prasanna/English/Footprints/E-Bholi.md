# E-Bholi

> **Footprints Without Feet — Ch 8** | Author: **K.A. Abbas** | [[📖 English]]

## Theme
Education empowers women, overcoming prejudice and physical flaws

## Summary
Bholi (Sulekha) was a slow learner, had pockmarks from smallpox, and stammered. Her parents neglected her. A teacher at the village school treated her with love and encouraged her to speak. Years later, her parents arranged her marriage to an old, greedy man named Bishamber. During the wedding, seeing her pockmarks, he demands 5000 rupees dowry. Her father pleads, but Bishamber is adamant. Empowered by her education, Bholi refuses to marry the greedy coward and decides to serve her parents and teach in the school instead.

## Key Points
- **Bholi** means simpleton. Real name: Sulekha.
- **Teacher's role**: Transformed a fearful, stammering girl into a confident woman. A masterpiece of a teacher.
- **Bishamber**: Greedy, heartless, materialistic.
- Education gave her a voice and self-respect.

## Exam Q Patterns
- SA: Why was Sulekha called Bholi?
- SA: How did Bholi's teacher play an important role in changing the course of her life?
- LA: Bholi's real ornament was her education, not her marriage. Discuss.
