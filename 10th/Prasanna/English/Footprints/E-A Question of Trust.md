# E-A Question of Trust

> **Footprints Without Feet — Ch 4** | Author: **Victor Canning** | [[📖 English]]

## Theme
Honor among thieves, appearance can be deceptive

## Summary
Horace Danby is a respectable locksmith but steals a safe once a year to buy rare books. While robbing the Shotover Grange, he meets a young, confident lady in red who acts like the owner's wife. She asks him to open the safe for her because she "forgot the numbers," promising not to report him. He opens it without gloves and leaves. Later, he is arrested — his fingerprints are everywhere. The lady in red was actually another thief!

## Key Points
- **Horace Danby**: 50, unmarried, suffers from hay fever, meticulous planner
- **The Lady in Red**: young, clever, outsmarts the experienced thief
- Irony: He gets caught not for his own theft, but for helping another thief. There is no "honor among thieves."

## Exam Q Patterns
- SA: Why did Horace Danby rob a safe every year?
- SA: How did the lady in red deceive Horace?
- LA: Do you think Horace Danby was unfairly punished? Why?
