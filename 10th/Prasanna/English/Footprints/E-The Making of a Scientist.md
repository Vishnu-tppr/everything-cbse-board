# E-The Making of a Scientist

> **Footprints Without Feet — Ch 6** | Author: **Robert W. Peterson** | [[📖 English]]

## Theme
Curiosity, maternal support, and continuous learning lead to success

## Summary
Richard Ebright was an only child who loved collecting butterflies. His mother encouraged his curiosity, bought him equipment, and gave him a book, *The Travels of Monarch X*. This opened the world of science for him. He lost at his first science fair, which taught him that real science is about experiments, not just neat displays. He later discovered the purpose of the 12 gold spots on a monarch pupa (they produce a hormone necessary for growth), leading to his groundbreaking work on cell DNA.

## Key Points
- **Ebright's ingredients for a scientist**: a first-rate mind, curiosity, and the will to win for the right reasons.
- **Mother's role**: His driving force, bought him telescope/microscope, took him on trips, got him the pivotal book.
- **Dr. Urquhart**: Provided ideas for experiments.
- Realizing the difference between displaying things (losing) and doing real experiments (winning).

## Exam Q Patterns
- SA: How did Ebright's mother help him?
- SA: What lesson did Ebright learn when he didn't win at the science fair?
- LA: What are the essential qualities that go into the making of a scientist?
