# E-A Letter to God

> **First Flight — Ch 1** | Author: **G.L. Fuentes** | [[📖 English]]

## Theme
Unshakeable faith in God, irony of situation

## Summary
Lencho, a poor farmer, writes a letter to God asking for 100 pesos after a hailstorm destroys his crops. The postmaster and employees collect 70 pesos and send it as "from God." Lencho receives it but writes another letter complaining that God sent only 70 — accusing the post office employees of stealing 30 pesos.

## Key Points
- Lencho's complete, innocent faith in God
- The **irony**: the very people who helped him are accused of stealing
- Post office employees' kindness goes unrecognized

## Important Quotes
- "A plague of locusts would have left more than this"
- "God could not have made a mistake, nor could he have denied Lencho what he had requested"

- Extract-based: identify speaker, context
- SA (3M): Why did Lencho write a second letter?
- SA (3M): How did the post office employees help Lencho?
- Value-based: What does the story teach about faith and gratitude?
