# E-Nelson Mandela

> **First Flight — Ch 2** | Author: **Nelson Mandela** | [[📖 English]]

## Theme
Freedom, courage, sacrifice, reconciliation

## Summary
Mandela reflects on the inauguration day of South Africa's first democratic government. He recalls the long struggle against apartheid, the sacrifices of countless freedom fighters, and his vision of a rainbow nation where all races live in harmony.

## Key Points
- Inauguration: 10 May 1994
- "Apartheid" = racial segregation policy in South Africa
- Mandela's twin obligations: to family and to country
- Courage = not absence of fear, but triumph over it
- Greatest wealth of South Africa = its people

## Important Quotes
- "For to be free is not merely to cast off one's chains, but to live in a way that respects and enhances the freedom of others"
- "The brave man is not he who does not feel afraid, but he who conquers that fear"

- Extract-based: identify context of Mandela's speech
- SA: What did Mandela mean by "twin obligations"?
- LA: How did Mandela's understanding of freedom change over time?
