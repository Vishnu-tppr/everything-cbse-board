# E-The Sermon at Benares

> **First Flight — Ch 8** | Author: **Betty Renshaw** | [[📖 English]]

## Theme
Death is inevitable, acceptance, impermanence of life

## Summary
Gautama Buddha encounters old age, sickness, and death. He renounces worldly life, attains enlightenment. A grieving mother, Kisa Gotami, comes to him with her dead son, asking for medicine. Buddha asks her to bring mustard seeds from a house where no one has died. She finds no such house and understands: death is universal.

## Key Points
- Siddhartha Gautama → became the Buddha (the Enlightened One)
- Kisa Gotami's grief → lesson about universality of death
- Mustard seed parable: clever teaching method
- "The life of mortals is brief and combined with pain"
- Message: don't grieve for the dead; accept death as natural

## Exam Q Patterns
- Extract: Kisa Gotami's search for mustard seeds
- SA: What lesson did Kisa Gotami learn?
- LA: How did Buddha teach about death without directly preaching?
