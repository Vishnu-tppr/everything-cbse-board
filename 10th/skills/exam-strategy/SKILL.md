## Exam-Day Time Management (3-hour, 80M paper)

| Phase | Time | Activity |
|-------|------|----------|
| Reading time | 15 min | Read full paper, plan approach |
| MCQs | 30-35 min | Attempt all, mark uncertain |
| Short Answers | 60-70 min | Show steps, draw diagrams |
| Long Answers | 40-50 min | Given/To Find/Solution format |
| Case Study | 20-25 min | Read passage, extract data |
| Review | 10-15 min | Check unanswered, silly errors |

## Golden Rules Across Subjects

1. **Never leave a question blank** — partial answers get partial marks
2. **Show ALL working steps** — marks are in the process
3. **Draw diagrams even when not asked** — earns bonus clarity
4. **Write units always** — cm, cm², Ω, V, etc.
5. **Underline final answers and key terms**
6. **Start each section on fresh page**
7. **Double-check question numbers**

## Presentation Tips

- Clear handwriting (practiced at exam speed)
- Use headings: Given, To Find, Solution, Therefore
- Diagrams with ruler, properly labeled
- Leave 2-3 line gaps between answers
- Box/underline final answers

## Internal Assessment — 20/20 Strategy

| Component | Marks | Tips |
|-----------|-------|------|
| Periodic Tests (best 2/3) | 10 | Treat as mini-boards |
| Notebook | 5 | Complete, neat, dated, indexed |
| Subject Enrichment | 5 | Full participation, neat presentation |

## Study Habits & Wellness

- **45 min focused study → 10 min break** (Pomodoro)
- Sleep 7-8 hours — NEVER sacrifice sleep
- Active recall: close book, write from memory, check
- Teach concepts to someone — best learning method
- Alternate subjects — don't study one subject all day
- Exercise 30 min daily, eat balanced, stay hydrated
- **Positive self-talk**: "I have prepared well. I will do well."
