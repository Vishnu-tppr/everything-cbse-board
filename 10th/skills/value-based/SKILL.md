# Value-Based Questions — Easy Marks Most Students Miss

## What Are Value-Based Questions?
```
"Raju noticed his neighbor burning garbage in the open.
 He politely explained the harmful effects and suggested alternatives.
 (a) What values does Raju display? (1M)
 (b) Mention two harmful effects of burning garbage. (2M)
 (c) Suggest two environment-friendly alternatives. (2M)"

Total: 5M for basic knowledge + identifying values
These appear in Science and SST papers.
```

## The Formula for Answering Value-Based Qs
```
STEP 1: Identify the VALUE being tested (1M)
  → Environmental awareness, social responsibility, civic sense,
    empathy, scientific temperament, national integration

STEP 2: Answer the CONCEPT part (2-3M)
  → Use NCERT knowledge as usual

STEP 3: Connect VALUE + CONCEPT (1M)
  → "This shows environmental awareness because [NCERT concept]"
```

## Common Values Tested in CBSE

### Science Values
```
Environmental awareness: pollution, conservation, waste management
Scientific temperament: questioning, evidence-based thinking
Health consciousness: balanced diet, exercise, hygiene
Social responsibility: blood donation, organ donation, vaccination
Sustainable development: renewable energy, reduce-reuse-recycle
```

### SST Values
```
National integration: unity in diversity, secularism
Democratic values: participation, accountability, transparency
Gender equality: equal rights, education for all
Economic awareness: saving, consumer rights, fair trade
Constitutional values: fundamental rights and duties
```

## Example Answers by Subject

### Science Value-Based
```
Q: "Priya's family installed solar panels on their roof and encouraged
    neighbors to do the same."
A: (a) Values: Environmental consciousness, community leadership,
       and commitment to sustainable development.
   (b) Benefits of solar energy:
       (i) Solar energy is renewable and does not cause pollution.
       (ii) It reduces dependence on fossil fuels which are non-renewable.
   (c) Priya shows social responsibility by not only adopting clean
       energy but also spreading awareness in her community, contributing
       to reducing carbon emissions and climate change.
```

### SST Value-Based
```
Q: "During elections, Ravi noticed that many young voters were not going
    to vote. He organized an awareness campaign."
A: (a) Values: Democratic participation, civic responsibility, and
       awareness of fundamental duties.
   (b) Importance of voting:
       (i) Voting is a fundamental right that strengthens democracy.
       (ii) Higher voter turnout ensures better representation.
   (c) As per Article 326 of the Constitution, every citizen above 18
       has the right to vote. It is our duty under Article 51A to
       participate in the democratic process.
```

## Key Tip
```
These questions are GIFT marks. They're the easiest 5M on the paper.
Most students write vague answers like "Raju is a good person."
Write SPECIFIC values + NCERT concepts = full marks.
```
